# 二叔(上)

**Date:** 2025-05-31 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121251/8761772?zh-cn

清朝晚期，我的爷爷在京城里当武官，慈禧皇太后任命爷爷为北洋水师提督一职。

爷爷小时候家境贫寒，念过几年私塾，文化不高，但他胆子大，性情猛烈。他的思维方式也与众不同，从小就喜欢习武，用树枝和木棍当作大刀和长矛，经常在院子里冲锋上阵地摆弄他的「兵器」。他会抽空到几里路远的武术馆去偷看别人练武，回家后学样练习，逐渐地学到了很多本领。

一次，武术馆的学员进行比赛，爷爷也直冲猛撞地上去打起了擂台，师傅见到爷爷有非常精湛的武艺，笑着说：「你会有出息的。」

爷爷的武艺出名以后，进京成了一名武官，他长得人高马大、性格刚烈，更能出征打仗，深得慈禧皇太后的赏识。爷爷常说：「我天不怕，地不怕，每次出征，我都是用热肚皮对着凉刀剑。」他一生战果辉煌、旗开得胜、硕果累累，是位勇敢、优秀的将领。

我的二叔就是出生在这个官宦之家，性情酷像爷爷，打小就混身是胆，天不怕地不怕，没有人能管得住他、压得下他。他每天不是爬树就是翻墙，一身的劲没地方使，无奈，爷爷让他去报名参军。

二叔来到兵营参加军训操练，夜间行军，射枪搏斗，从不叫苦叫累。在聆听兵法授课时，他领悟得又快又好，他会把刚学到的兵法融入到实战中去，真是活学活用，立竿见影。

一次炎夏，大家在酷热的太阳底下练操，经过暴晒的同学们都头晕目眩，一名同学昏倒在地，二叔见状，二话没说，背起同学奔向医务室，刚踏进医务室大门，二人一起都倒下……。

同样有一次，兵团的长官看见一个新入伍的士兵在练习兵法时，几次三番动作不规范，长官就不耐烦、粗暴大声吼斥，用不雅之词训斥他；之后长官还把椅子和很重的琐碎东西，都让那名新兵背在身上走，以此来惩罚他。

二叔见此，心中很不爽，就壮着胆子为那小兵说话，对长官说：「你对待小兵的态度不对，有话好好说，不能欺负新兵。」长官听后大怒：「什么东西，敢和我擡杠？岂有此理，说话的人是谁？」二叔马上从队伍中站出来，举起右手，行着军礼：「报告，是我，我叫彭长鸣。」长官用眼睛飘了二叔一眼，说：「你长了豹子胆，教训我来了。」

二叔没有声音，长官动怒了，狠狠地训斥二叔。这下二叔耿直、豪爽的脾气也上来了，不顾后果，对着长官凶巴巴地说：「不用多话，有种的下水比高低。」一面说，一边把自己的军衣一件件扒光，军鞋一扔，摆出一副势不可挡的气势，让长官下水说话。

光脚的不怕穿鞋的，长官相当尴尬，说真的，他哪是二叔的对手，臃肿的身体那能敌得过年轻、少壮的士兵？正在骑虎难下的气氛中，不料，那个新兵撒腿跑到长官面前跪地磕头，哀求地说：「长官，请原谅，都是我犯的错，我的不对，您千万不能下水去打他，也请您原谅他一次把，我让他上来向您赔礼道歉。」新兵还算机灵，化解了这尴尬的局面。

事后，长官思来想去，感觉这个名叫彭长鸣的小子是个人才，不但胆子大，而且品德也不错，好生培养，将来会有出息。虽然长官性情比较粗鲁，他还是很爱才的，主意一定，他推荐二叔去了黄埔军校学习。

二叔从排长一路升迁到团长，胸前别了很多威风凛凛的勋章，他继承了我爷爷的基因，血管里流淌着同样沸腾的热血。二叔混成了人样，没忘记当年推荐他上黄埔军校的长官，特地千里之外赶去问候他，感谢他的栽培之恩。

一九四八年，二叔手里拿着飞往台湾的[**机票**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/机票)路过上海，准备与母亲和大哥告别，随着[**国民党**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/国民党)的部队去台湾。没料到，我的祖母拉着二叔的手，哭天抢地不让走，说：「留下来，和大哥一起做瓷器生意，一家人团圆才是重要的。」他的大哥（我父亲）也配合母亲，一起劝说把二叔留下来。（上）

## Second Uncle (Part 1)
In the late Qing dynasty, my grandfather served as a military officer in the capital. Empress Dowager Cixi appointed him to the post of Admiral of the Beiyang Fleet.

During his childhood, my grandfather’s family was impoverished. He attended a private school for only a few years and did not have much of an education, but he was daring and fierce by nature. His way of thinking was also unconventional. From a young age, he loved martial arts, using tree branches and wooden sticks as sabers and spears, often charging around the courtyard to brandish his "weapons." He would sneak over to a martial arts school several miles away to watch others train, then practice what he saw when he returned home, gradually acquiring a formidable set of skills.

Once, during a competition at the martial arts school, my grandfather boldly stepped up to the arena to challenge the others. Seeing his exquisite martial arts skills, the master smiled and said, "You will go far."

After my grandfather became famous for his martial arts, he went to the capital and became a military officer. Tall, robust, and fierce, he was exceptionally capable in battle and won the deep appreciation of Empress Dowager Cixi. My grandfather often said, "I fear neither heaven nor earth. Every time I go into battle, I face cold blades with my warm belly." Throughout his life, his military achievements were glorious, winning every battle and yielding abundant successes. He was a brave and outstanding general.

My second uncle was born into this official family. His temperament closely resembled my grandfather's; since childhood, he was full of courage, fearing nothing, and no one could control or suppress him. Every day he was either climbing trees or scaling walls, with nowhere to expend his boundless energy. Left with no other choice, my grandfather had him sign up to join the military.

Second uncle went to the military camp to participate in drills. He never once complained of hardship or exhaustion during night marches, shooting practice, or hand-to-hand combat. When listening to lectures on military strategy, he grasped the concepts quickly and thoroughly, immediately integrating what he had just learned into actual combat. It was truly a case of active learning with instant results.

One sweltering summer day, everyone was drilling under the scorching sun. Baked by the intense heat, the cadets grew dizzy, and one of them collapsed to the ground. Seeing this, my second uncle, without a second word, hoisted the classmate onto his back and rushed to the infirmary. Just as he stepped through the door, they both collapsed...

On another occasion, a unit commander saw a newly enlisted soldier fail to perform standard movements correctly several times during military drills. Impatient and harsh, the commander roared in anger, scolding him with vulgar words. Afterward, he even forced the recruit to carry a chair and heavy miscellaneous items on his back as punishment.

Seeing this, my second uncle felt very displeased. He plucked up his courage to speak up for the young soldier, saying to the commander, "Your attitude toward the private is wrong. Speak properly, you shouldn't bully new recruits." The commander flew into a rage: "What kind of creature dares to argue with me? Ridiculous! Who is speaking?" My second uncle immediately stepped out of the ranks, raised his right hand, and saluted: "Reporting, sir, it is me. My name is Peng Changming." The commander glanced at my second uncle out of the corner of his eye and said, "You've got a lot of nerve, lecturing me."

My second uncle remained silent, which only angered the commander further, who harshly berated him. At this point, my second uncle's upright and forthright temper flared up. Disregarding the consequences, he said fiercely to the commander, "No need for more words. If you have the guts, let's go into the water and settle this." As he spoke, he stripped off his military uniform piece by piece, threw down his military boots, and struck an unstoppable pose, challenging the commander to get in the water and talk.

The barefoot has nothing to lose. The commander was thoroughly embarrassed; truth be told, he was no match for my second uncle. How could his flabby body compete with a young, vigorous soldier? While the atmosphere was tense and awkward, the recruit unexpectedly dashed to the commander, knelt down, and kowtowed, pleading, "Commander, please forgive us, it was all my fault. Please, you must not go into the water to fight him. Please forgive him just this once. I will have him come up and apologize to you." The recruit was quite clever and defused this embarrassing situation.

Afterward, the commander reflected on the incident and felt that this kid named Peng Changming was a rare talent. Not only was he bold, but he also possessed good moral character. With proper grooming, he would go far. Although the commander was somewhat rough around the edges, he highly valued talent. Having made up his mind, he recommended my second uncle to study at the Whampoa Military Academy.

My second uncle rose through the ranks from platoon leader to regiment commander, his chest adorned with many prestigious medals. He inherited my grandfather's genes, with the same boiling hot blood running through his veins. Having made a name for himself, my second uncle did not forget the commander who had recommended him to the Whampoa Military Academy. He traveled thousands of miles to pay him a visit and thank him for his guidance and nurturing.

In 1948, holding a plane ticket to Taiwan, my second uncle passed through Shanghai, planning to say goodbye to his mother and eldest brother before departing for Taiwan with the Nationalist troops. Unexpectedly, my grandmother held my second uncle's hand, crying hysterically and refusing to let him go, saying, "Stay behind and do porcelain business with your big brother. It is most important for the family to be together." His eldest brother (my father) joined their mother in persuading my second uncle to stay. (Part 1)
