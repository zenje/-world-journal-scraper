# 「小黄鱼」去哪儿(下)

**Date:** 2026-02-22 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121251/9321283?zh-cn

对门邻居是个人员众多的大家庭，家中过日子的生活来源，是靠两台冲床机帮助里弄加工厂加工，把铜片冲出各种形状，是给电器用品提供配件的，因此家中到处都有铜片的边角料。这家的老母亲手上也有不少「小黄鱼」，怕红卫兵抄家，整日都在忧愁，几个女儿和母亲商量讨论，总要想个法子避免这场灾难，决定让「小黄鱼」破相，放到冲床上压成不同规则的形状，混在铜片中蒙混过关，黄金和铜片颜色相似，一下子难以区分。

这个方法真还不错，次日，红卫兵敲锣打鼓来到他们家，每间房门都敞开，家中乱糟糟的，满地都有黄颜色的金属碎片。他们对红卫兵说：「你们尽管检查。」红卫兵冲向了卧室和客厅，仔细搜查了各处，拿走了一些摆饰和字画，匆匆收场了事。

一家人松了一口气，他们把「小黄鱼」回拢之后，老母亲想走过了灾难，不如把这些分发给女儿和媳妇。这本应是件好事，但在分配过程中，儿媳觉得分发不均匀、不合理，说自己得少了，闹起了矛盾。

一天，婆媳为了一点小事又吵架了，媳妇不依不饶地走在街上，大声嚷嚷：「你家的金元宝、金宝塔藏到哪儿去了，是不是都塞给女儿了？」故意栽赃、夸大其词地陷害婆婆。不料媳妇这一叫，红卫兵二次冲进他们家，这次没有防备，金银财宝全搜走了。从此婆媳深仇大恨，老死不相往来。

我的亲家公是从海外回国求学的侨生，他每月能收到父母资助读书的二十美元。在那个年代，二十美元可兑换人民币约一百二十元左右。亲家公还能获得从国外寄来的各种服装和日常用品，如眼镜、手表、皮带、皮鞋等，都属于高档的物质，生活过得很舒服。除了每个月的花费，亲家公手中积蓄了不少钱，他也挺会理财，每三个月都会去金店带回一条「小黄鱼」。成家后，就把这些都让亲家母保管。

如今，外面的形势那么紧张，他们又有海外关系，「小黄鱼」成了累赘，烫手的山芋怎么摆脱？搞得亲家母心神不安，思来想去最终想出了一个明智的办法，把这些洋货和「小黄鱼」都装进了一个箱子里，决心趁早把这些烫手的东西上交给国家。

她第二天上班时就带上了箱子，走进工作单位的革命委员会，说明来意，要和这些「封、资、修、帝」的东西彻底切割。革命委员会的头头看她态度端正，诚恳真实，拿出了封条贴在箱子上，两人都签上了名字，同时还表扬了亲家母，说她对文化大革命有觉悟，敢于对自己挑战、向自己革命，表现不错。受到了革委会头头高度表扬，亲家母摆脱了包袱，也感到轻松多了，谁也不知道她心里的真正想法是什么。

文化大革命结束后，一天单位里的领导打电话给亲家母，叫她去领回自己的箱子，亲家母喜出望外地提着箱子回家，撕去封条，里面的物品一件也没少，几条「小黄鱼」安稳地躺在原处。

她把这个故事讲述给亲朋好友、同事们听，大家都觉得她很有智能，怎么策画这个妙招的？她回答：「只要人宽心爽，思想上没负担，钱财和物质都是身外之物，在当时的压力下，我真心愿意抛弃那一切。」

## Where Did the "Little Yellow Croakers" Go? (Part 2)
The family across the hall was a large household with many members. Their livelihood depended on two press machines, which they used to help a neighborhood processing factory stamp copper sheets into various shapes to provide parts for electrical appliances. Consequently, scrap copper sheets were scattered everywhere in their home. The elderly mother of the family also held quite a few "little yellow croakers" (small gold bars). Fearing that the Red Guards would raid her home, she was plagued by anxiety all day long. Her daughters discussed the matter with her, trying to find a way to avoid this disaster. They decided to disfigure the "little yellow croakers" by using the press machine to stamp them into different irregular shapes, mixing them in with the copper scraps to pass off unnoticed. Gold and copper are similar in color, making them difficult to distinguish at a glance.

This method was indeed quite effective. The next day, when the Red Guards arrived at their home beating gongs and drums, all the doors of the rooms were wide open. The house was in disarray, with yellow metal fragments scattered all over the floor. The family told the Red Guards, "Feel free to search." The Red Guards rushed into the bedrooms and living room, meticulously searching everywhere, but in the end, they only took a few ornaments and calligraphies, wrapping up their search in a hurry.

The family breathed a sigh of relief. After gathering the "little yellow croakers" back together, the elderly mother thought that since the disaster had passed, she might as well distribute them among her daughters and daughter-in-law. This should have been a good thing, but during the distribution process, the daughter-in-law felt that the distribution was uneven and unfair, claiming she received less, which led to a conflict.

One day, the mother-in-law and daughter-in-law quarreled again over a trivial matter. Refusing to let it go, the daughter-in-law walked onto the street and shouted at the top of her lungs, "Where did you hide your family's gold ingots and gold pagodas? Did you give them all to your daughters?" she intentionally framed and exaggerated accusations against her mother-in-law. Unexpectedly, because of her shouting, the Red Guards raided their home for a second time. Caught off guard this time, all their gold, silver, and treasures were ransacked and seized. From then on, the mother-in-law and daughter-in-law harbored deep-seated hatred for each other, and never spoke to or visited each other again.

My father-in-law was an overseas Chinese student who returned to China to study. Every month, he received twenty US dollars from his parents to support his education. In those days, twenty US dollars could be exchanged for about one hundred and twenty yuan in RMB. He also received various clothing and daily necessities sent from abroad, such as glasses, watches, belts, and leather shoes. These were all high-end luxury goods, and he lived a very comfortable life. In addition to his monthly expenses, my father-in-law saved up a lot of money. He was also quite good at financial planning; every three months, he would visit a gold shop and bring back a "little yellow croaker." After he got married, he handed all of them over to my mother-in-law for safekeeping.

Now, the situation outside was so tense, and because they had overseas connections, the "little yellow croakers" became a burden. How could they get rid of this hot potato? It made my mother-in-law extremely anxious. After thinking it over repeatedly, she finally came up with a wise plan: she packed all these foreign goods and the "little yellow croakers" into a suitcase, determined to hand these hot items over to the state as early as possible.

The next day, she took the suitcase with her to work, walked into the office of her work unit's Revolutionary Committee, and explained her intention to completely sever ties with these objects of "feudalism, capitalism, revisionism, and imperialism." Seeing her upright, sincere, and genuine attitude, the head of the Revolutionary Committee took out a paper seal, affixed it to the suitcase, and both of them signed their names on it. The head also praised my mother-in-law, saying she was highly conscious of the Cultural Revolution, dared to challenge herself, and revolted against her own self, showing excellent performance. Having received high praise from the head of the Revolutionary Committee, my mother-in-law shook off her burden and felt much lighter. No one knew what her true thoughts were.

After the Cultural Revolution ended, one day the leadership at her work unit called my mother-in-law, asking her to come and retrieve her suitcase. Overjoyed, she carried the suitcase home and tore off the seal. Not a single item inside was missing; the few "little yellow croakers" lay safely in their original place.

She shared this story with her relatives, friends, and colleagues. Everyone thought she was very clever and asked how she had planned such a brilliant move. She replied, "As long as one is broad-minded and free of mental burdens, wealth and material possessions are just external things. Under the pressure at that time, I was truly willing to give it all up."
