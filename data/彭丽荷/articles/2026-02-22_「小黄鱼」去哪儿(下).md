她第二天上班时就带上了箱子，走进工作单位的革命委员会，说明来意，要和这些「封、资、修、帝」的东西彻底切割。革命委员会的头头看她态度端正，诚恳真实，拿出了封条贴在箱子上，两人都签上了名字，同时还表扬了亲家母，说她对文化大革命有觉悟，敢于对自己挑战、向自己革命，表现不错。受到了革委会头头高度表扬，亲家母摆脱了包袱，也感到轻松多了，谁也不知道她心里的真正想法是什么。

文化大革命结束后，一天单位里的领导打电话给亲家母，叫她去领回自己的箱子，亲家母喜出望外地提着箱子回家，撕去封条，里面的物品一件也没少，几条「小黄鱼」安稳地躺在原处。

她把这个故事讲述给亲朋好友、同事们听，大家都觉得她很有智能，怎么策画这个妙招的？她回答：「只要人宽心爽，思想上没负担，钱财和物质都是身外之物，在当时的压力下，我真心愿意抛弃那一切。」

## English Translation
# Where Did the "Little Yellow Croakers" Go? (Part 2)

**Date:** 2026-02-22 01:00 ET  
**Author:** Peng Lihe  

Our neighbors across the way were a large family. Their livelihood depended on two punch presses they used to help a neighborhood processing workshop stamp copper sheets into various shapes, providing accessories for electrical appliances. As a result, scraps of copper sheets were scattered everywhere in their home. The elderly mother of this family also had quite a few "little yellow croakers" (small gold bars). Fearing that the Red Guards would raid her home, she was filled with anxiety all day long. Her daughters discussed the matter with her, trying to find a way to avoid this disaster. They decided to disfigure the "little yellow croakers" by using the punch press to stamp them into different irregular shapes, then mixing them in with the copper scraps to pass them off unnoticed. Since gold and copper are similar in color, it would be difficult to tell them apart at a glance.

This method turned out to be quite effective. The next day, the Red Guards arrived at their home beating gongs and drums. All the room doors were wide open, and the house was a mess, with yellow metal fragments scattered all over the floor. The family told the Red Guards, "Feel free to search." The Red Guards rushed into the bedrooms and living room, searched everywhere thoroughly, took away some ornaments, calligraphy, and paintings, and left in a hurry.

The family breathed a sigh of relief. After gathering the "little yellow croakers" back together, the elderly mother thought that since they had survived the disaster, she might as well distribute them to her daughters and daughters-in-law. This should have been a good thing, but during the distribution process, the daughter-in-law felt that the distribution was uneven and unfair, claiming she had received less, which led to a conflict.

One day, the mother-in-law and daughter-in-law quarreled again over a trivial matter. The daughter-in-law, refusing to back down, walked out onto the street and shouted loudly, "Where did you hide your family's gold ingots and gold pagodas? Did you give them all to your daughters?" She deliberately framed and exaggerated the situation to implicate her mother-in-law. Unexpectedly, because of her shouting, the Red Guards raided their home a second time. This time, caught off guard, all their gold, silver, and treasures were confiscated. From then on, the mother-in-law and daughter-in-law harbored a deep-seated hatred for each other and never associated with one another again for the rest of their lives.

My child's father-in-law was an overseas student who had returned to China to study. Every month, he received twenty US dollars from his parents to support his education. In those days, twenty US dollars could be exchanged for about 120 RMB. He also received various clothing items and daily necessities sent from abroad, such as glasses, watches, belts, and leather shoes, which were all considered high-end luxury goods, allowing him to live very comfortably. In addition to his monthly expenses, he accumulated a lot of savings. He was also quite good at managing money, and every three months, he would bring home a "little yellow croaker" from the gold shop. After he got married, he handed all of them over to his wife (my child's mother-in-law) for safekeeping.

Now, with the situation outside so tense and their overseas connections, the "little yellow croakers" became a liability. How could they rid themselves of this hot potato? It made my child's mother-in-law deeply anxious. After thinking it over, she finally came up with a wise solution: she packed all these foreign goods and the "little yellow croakers" into a chest, determined to hand these hot potatoes over to the state as early as possible.

The next day when she went to work, she brought the chest with her, walked into the Revolutionary Committee of her workplace, and explained her intention to make a clean break from these "feudal, capitalist, revisionist, and imperialist" things. Seeing her correct, sincere, and genuine attitude, the head of the Revolutionary Committee put a seal on the chest, and both of them signed their names. At the same time, he praised my child's mother-in-law, saying she showed great awareness of the Cultural Revolution, and had the courage to challenge and revolutionize herself, performing remarkably well. Highly praised by the head of the Revolutionary Committee, she shook off her burden and felt much lighter. No one knew what her true thoughts were.

After the Cultural Revolution ended, one day the leaders of her workplace called her to come and retrieve her chest. Overjoyed, she brought the chest home and tore off the seal. Not a single item inside was missing, and the few "little yellow croakers" were lying safely in their original place.

She shared this story with her relatives, friends, and colleagues, and everyone thought she was highly intelligent. How did she plan such a clever move? She replied, "As long as one has an open and cheerful mind, free of mental burdens, money and material wealth are just external things. Under the pressure at that time, I was truly willing to give it all up."
