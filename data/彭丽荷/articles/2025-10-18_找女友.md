# 找女友

**Date:** 2025-10-18 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121251/9063574?zh-cn

我和玲达、陆立人夫妇是多年的好邻居，在同一幢大楼里比邻而居，不论发生什么大小事，都会互相倾诉、互相帮助，因此两家的情况彼此都很清楚。

他俩原在同一所高校读书时相识的，陆立人比玲达大四岁，在数学系读硕士学位，而玲达则是本科文学班的二年级学生。陆立人比较懦弱、胆小怕事，一股十足的书生气，玲达性格坚强直爽，人也能干，而且吃得起苦。他俩的性格正好能起到互补作用。两年之后，陆立人留校任教，玲达分配到一所中学教语文课。两人都有了稳定的工作，收入也不错，在双方父母催促下，走进了婚姻殿堂。婚后，夫妇恩爱有加，生活美满幸福。

一天，玲达告知丈夫她有身孕了，陆立人兴奋得连忙把妻子抱起来，在客厅中转了三圈，并高呼：「我要当爸爸了，我想要个儿子。」喜悦之情洋溢仰整个面庞。

上帝没有辜负他们的期望，一个白白胖胖的小子来到他们家。这小子似乎特别聪明，脑壳大大的，额头高高的，眼睛圆圆的，乌黑的双眸不停地在眼眶里打转，似乎总在动脑筋、想问题。陆立人说，叫他聪儿吧。

三岁的聪儿和父母参加了一个派对，晚上回家只见他不太对劲，整个身子软绵绵的，夫妻俩赶快让聪儿喝点白开水，上床睡觉。半夜玲达感到身边热呼呼的，她把面额贴近聪儿的额头，不对，聪儿发烧了。

夫妻二人赶紧把聪儿送到医院，孩子一直处在昏昏沉沉的状态，经过各种检查，几天以后，诊断为「脑膜炎」，他们被这突然其来的意外打蒙了。聪儿高烧一直不退，逐渐进入昏迷状态，经过各种治疗都不管用，怎么办？

在医院躺了十多天，高烧不退，聪儿仍然昏迷不醒，每天挂盐水、抗生素加退热药。夫妇二人急得像热锅上的蚂蚁，又似无头苍蝇，乱转、瞎撞。

一星期过去了，聪儿体温开始一点一点下降，但神志不清，一双大眼睛呆呆地盯着一处，一动也不动。玲达在旁边调用着：「聪儿、聪儿，你看看我，看看我。」孩子没有任何反应。

又过了十天，情况依旧，没有一丝好转迹象，又因为每天的[**医疗费**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/医疗费)用昂贵，夫妻也筋疲力尽，陆立人和玲达商量后，想把孩子带回家。医师说：「得脑膜炎的儿童，有些也能恢复，如果恢复得好，会比原来更聪明。当然有些孩子也会智力下降，严重的就是傻子。」夫妇二人怀着忧虑的心情，踏着沉重的脚步，把孩子带回了家。

一个月、两个月过去了，聪儿的病情未见好转。一年过后，陆立人觉得从此生活暗淡无光，思想包袱和心情的压抑，严重影响了他正常生活，他产生了放弃聪儿的想法，而玲达坚决不同意，一心要治疗好孩子。从此，夫妇二人争吵不断，矛盾愈来愈大，陆立人最后离开了这个家。

重重困难落到玲达一人肩上，她每天要带孩子康复治疗，不管天晴下雨、寒冬酷暑，没有中断过一次。她还要多找一份工作，赚钱来支付聪儿的治疗费用。一下子，玲达憔悴了不少，但她仍然坚持一个信念：聪儿会好起来的。（上）

## Looking for a Girlfriend
My husband and I have been good neighbors with Linda and Lu Liren for many years. Living next door to each other in the same building, we confided in and helped each other no matter what happened, big or small. Therefore, both families knew each other's situations very well.

The two of them met while studying at the same university. Lu Liren was four years older than Linda and was pursuing a master's degree in the mathematics department, while Linda was a sophomore in the undergraduate literature department. Lu Liren was relatively weak-willed and timid, possessing a classic bookish air, whereas Linda had a strong and straightforward personality, was capable, and could endure hardships. Their personalities complemented each other perfectly. Two years later, Lu Liren remained at the university to teach, and Linda was assigned to teach Chinese at a middle school. With stable jobs and decent incomes, they walked down the aisle under the urging of both sets of parents. After marriage, the couple loved each other deeply, living a blissful and happy life.

One day, Linda informed her husband that she was pregnant. Excitedly, Lu Liren immediately picked up his wife, spun her around three times in the living room, and shouted, "I'm going to be a father! I want a son!" Joy radiated across his entire face.

God did not let them down. A chubby, fair-skinned baby boy arrived in their family. This little guy seemed exceptionally smart, with a large head, a high forehead, and round eyes. His dark pupils constantly darted around in their sockets, as if he was always thinking and analyzing things. Lu Liren said, "Let's call him Cong'er."

At three years old, Cong'er attended a party with his parents. When they returned home that evening, he didn't seem quite right; his entire body was limp. The couple quickly had him drink some warm water and put him to bed. In the middle of the night, Linda felt a warmth beside her. She pressed her cheek to Cong'er's forehead—no, Cong'er had a fever.

The couple rushed Cong'er to the hospital. The child remained in a daze. After various examinations, he was diagnosed with meningitis a few days later, a sudden blow that left them completely stunned. Cong'er's high fever refused to break, and he gradually fell into a coma. None of the treatments worked. What were they to do?

After lying in the hospital for more than ten days, Cong'er's fever still had not broken, and he remained comatose. Every day, he was put on IV drips, antibiotics, and fever reducers. The couple was as anxious as ants on a hot pan, running around blindly like headless flies.

A week passed, and Cong'er's temperature slowly began to drop, but he remained unconscious, his big eyes staring blankly at one spot without moving. Linda called out beside him, "Cong'er, Cong'er, look at me, look at me." The child had no response whatsoever.

Another ten days went by, and the situation remained unchanged with no sign of improvement. Due to the expensive daily medical costs and their own utter exhaustion, Lu Liren and Linda consulted with each other and decided to take the child home. The doctor said, "Some children who contract meningitis can recover; if they recover well, they might even be smarter than before. Of course, some children may experience a decline in intelligence, and in severe cases, become mentally disabled." With anxious hearts and heavy steps, the couple took their child back home.

One month, then two months passed, yet Cong'er's condition showed no improvement. A year later, Lu Liren felt that his life had become completely dark and bleak. The mental burden and suppressed emotions severely affected his daily life, leading him to harbor thoughts of giving up on Cong'er. However, Linda adamantly disagreed, determined to cure her child. From then on, the couple argued constantly, and their conflicts escalated until Lu Liren finally walked out on the family.

The heavy burden fell entirely onto Linda's shoulders. She had to take her child for rehabilitation therapy every day, never missing a session regardless of rain or shine, bitter winter or scorching summer. She also had to take on a second job to earn money to pay for Cong'er's treatment. Almost overnight, Linda looked much older and haggard, but she still held onto a single belief: Cong'er would get better. (Part 1)
