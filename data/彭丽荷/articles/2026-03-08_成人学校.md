# 成人学校

**Date:** 2026-03-08 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷

Wigan大约六十岁左右，眼角有一些鱼尾纹，金黄的短发中夹带几丝银发，炯炯有神的蓝色眼珠深深地嵌在眼眶里，以犀利目光观察着每个学员。Wigan一辈子从事教学工作，讲课条理清晰、思路敏捷，举一反三，总会站在学员的角度，用最简单、最接地气的方法，让学员们一听就懂、一懂即能应用。加上她丰富的肢体动作，大大提高了学员们的学习兴趣和主动性，大家都十分爱戴且尊重她。

## English Translation
Adult School

Date: March 8, 2026, 1:00 AM ET
Author: Peng Lihe

The adult school in the Carmel Valley community of San Diego utilizes classrooms borrowed from a local elementary school. Three days a week, the teacher, Ms. Wigan, stands at the school gate holding a small red flag. With a smile on her face, she gathers the arriving adult English learners into a neat line. Once everyone is assembled, she waves her hand, signaling it is time to head into the classroom.

Ms. Wigan is about sixty years old, with a few crow's feet at the corners of her eyes, and streaks of silver running through her short golden hair. Her bright, piercing blue eyes are set deep in their sockets, observing each student with a sharp gaze. Having dedicated her life to teaching, Ms. Wigan delivers highly organized, quick-witted lessons. She is adept at illustrating concepts through multiple examples, always putting herself in the students' shoes to explain things in the simplest, most practical way possible, so that students understand instantly and can apply the knowledge right away. Coupled with her expressive body language, this greatly enhances the students' interest and initiative in learning, and everyone loves and respects her deeply.

A diverse group of students of all ages and ethnicities sit in their designated seats, habitually taking out their notebooks and ballpoint pens to copy down the lesson text that Ms. Wigan has written on the blackboard beforehand. Once the students finish copying, Ms. Wigan asks everyone to look at the board and read aloud the dialogue for characters A and B, and then has the class repeat it loudly several times.

She divides the students into roles A and B to practice the dialogue. Once the students have mastered these English conversations, they take turns in groups to perform on stage. Everyone gets a chance to showcase their speaking skills, which helps build their confidence to speak English boldly. Sometimes Ms. Wigan even takes us to a second-grade classroom to pair up the elementary schoolers with the adult students. The young children act as teachers, earnestly asking questions, while the older students rack their brains, slowly stumbling through their answers. Through these various training methods, the students' English speaking skills have progressed rapidly; even those who used to be reluctant to open their mouths have now become eager, active participants. After a school year of hard work, everyone has reaped great rewards, and each person is able to converse in English. Seeing this, both Ms. Wigan and the students smile with pride.

Ms. Wigan is not only an effective teacher, but she also cares deeply for her students. Once, while picking up my sister at the airport, I accidentally fell and fractured my right leg. I called Ms. Wigan to tell her I couldn't attend class for the time being, adding, 'I won't be able to hear you teach, and my heart will always be lingering in the classroom.'

Monday was supposed to be a class day. I sat in my chair, feeling incredibly frustrated and helpless. At eleven o'clock in the morning, the doorbell suddenly rang, and Ms. Wigan appeared at my doorstep, much to my surprise and delight. She reached out her warm hands, carefully and gently helping me into her car. Moved to tears, I didn't know how to express my gratitude to her. After school, Ms. Wigan drove me back home and told me not to worry about getting to school in the future—she would always help me. She laughed and joked, 'I am your personal driver now.'

Once, the person knocking on the door was not Ms. Wigan, but a substitute teacher who came to pick me up. The substitute teacher smiled and told me, 'Ms. Wigan's mother is having surgery, so she couldn't make it to class. But she urged me that there is a student in the class who needs transportation, and I must not forget her. Not a single student can be left behind.'

At the graduation ceremony, I bought a bouquet of fresh flowers and a beautiful shawl. I draped the shawl over Ms. Wigan's shoulders with both hands, handed her the flowers, and bowed to express my gratitude. When our eyes met, they shone with a beautiful radiance.
