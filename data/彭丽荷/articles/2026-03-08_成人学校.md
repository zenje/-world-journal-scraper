# 成人学校

**Date:** 2026-03-08 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷

圣地亚牙卡美尔谷（Carmel Valley,San Diego）社区的成人学校，是借用一所小学的教室开展教学工作的。每周三天，老师Wigan手拿小红旗站在学校门前，面带笑容召集前来学习英语的成人们列队站好，等学生们到齐后，她大手一挥，示意要进教室了。

Wigan大约六十岁左右，眼角有一些鱼尾纹，金黄的短发中夹带几丝银发，炯炯有神的蓝色眼珠深深地嵌在眼眶里，以犀利目光观察着每个学员。Wigan一辈子从事教学工作，讲课条理清晰、思路敏捷，举一反三，总会站在学员的角度，用最简单、最接地气的方法，让学员们一听就懂、一懂即能应用。加上她丰富的肢体动作，大大提高了学员们的学习兴趣和主动性，大家都十分爱戴且尊重她。

一大群男女老少、不同肤色的学员们坐在指定座位上，习惯地拿出笔记本和原子笔，把Wigan事先写在黑板上的课文内容抄写下来。等学员们抄写完毕，Wigan要求大家注视黑板，诵读A、B两个角色对话的内容，然后让同学们高声反复读几遍。

她将学员们分别以A、B角色练习对话，当学员们掌握这些英语会话后，再一组一组轮流到讲台上去表演，个个都有机会展示自己的口才，同时培养学员们大胆开口讲英文的信心。有时Wigan还带我们去小学二年级的教室，让小学生与学员组成一对一对的老小搭子，小学生成为老师的角色，认真地问话，老学员口笨拙舌地边思索边慢慢回答。通过各种方法的训练，学员们英语口说能力进步飞速，原来不肯开口说话的人，也成了抢先发言的好学生。经过一学年的努力学习，大家收获满满，每个人都能用英语会话交流。看到这一切，Wigan和学员们都得意地笑了。

Wigan不仅教学有方，也非常关心学员。一次我去机场接我的姊妹，不慎摔跤，右腿骨折。我打电话告诉Wigan暂时不能去学校上课，又说：「我不能听到您上课的声音，心中老是惦挂着教室。」

星期一原本是应该要上课的日子，我坐在椅子上极度沮丧且无奈，早上十一时，门铃突然响了，Wigan出现在我家门口，真是令我喜出望外。她伸出温暖的双手，悉心体贴地牵扶我坐上她的车，我激动得热泪盈眶，不知该怎么表达我对她的感激。放学后，Wigan又送我回家，并且告诉我，以后去学校不用担心，她都会帮助我的，并笑着打趣：「我现在是你的专用司机。」

有一次，敲门的不是Wigan，而是一名代课老师前来接我。代课老师面带微笑地告诉我：「Wigan的母亲开刀，无法来上课。她叮嘱我班里有一名需要接送的学生，千万不能忘记她，学员一个不能少、一个也不能让他掉队。」

结业典礼时，我买了一束鲜花和一条漂亮披肩，我双手把披肩盖在Wigan的肩膀上，鲜花送到她手中，向她鞠躬致谢。当我俩的视线交织在一起的时候，绽放出美丽的神采。

## English Translation
# Adult School

**Date:** 2026-03-08 01:00 ET
**Author:** Peng Lihe

The adult school in the Carmel Valley, San Diego community conducts its classes by borrowing classrooms from a local elementary school. Three days a week, the teacher, Ms. Wigan, stands in front of the school gate with a small red flag in her hand. With a smile on her face, she gathers the adults who have come to learn English and lines them up. Once all the students have arrived, she waves her hand, signaling that it is time to enter the classroom.

Wigan is about sixty years old, with some crow's feet at the corners of her eyes. Her short, golden hair is streaked with a few strands of silver, and her bright, piercing blue eyes are deeply set in their sockets, observing each student with a sharp gaze. Having dedicated her entire life to teaching, Wigan delivers well-structured lectures with a quick mind, drawing broad lessons from single examples. She always puts herself in the students' shoes, using the simplest and most down-to-earth methods so that the students understand instantly and can apply what they learn immediately. Coupled with her rich body language, this greatly enhances the students' interest and initiative in learning, and everyone deeply loves and respects her.

A large group of students of all ages and skin colors sit in their designated seats, habitually taking out their notebooks and ballpoint pens to copy down the text that Wigan has written on the blackboard beforehand. Once the students finish copying, Wigan asks everyone to look closely at the blackboard and read aloud the dialogue between characters A and B, and then has the classmates read it aloud repeatedly.

She has the students practice the dialogue as characters A and B. Once the students master these English conversations, they take turns going up to the podium in groups to perform. Everyone gets a chance to showcase their eloquence, which also builds their confidence to speak English boldly. Sometimes, Wigan even takes us to a second-grade classroom to pair up elementary school students and adult students. The children act as teachers, asking questions seriously, while the older students, tongue-tied, ponder and reply slowly. Through various training methods, the students' English speaking skills have progressed rapidly; even those who were previously unwilling to open their mouths have become eager participants who vie to speak first. After a school year of hard work, everyone has reaped a rich harvest, and each person can communicate in English. Seeing all of this, Wigan and the students all smile with pride.

Wigan is not only an effective teacher but also deeply cares for her students. Once, while going to the airport to pick up my sister, I accidentally fell and fractured my right leg. I called Wigan to tell her that I wouldn't be able to attend school for the time being, adding, "Since I won't be able to hear you teach, I'll always be thinking about the classroom."

Monday was originally supposed to be a school day. I sat in my chair, feeling extremely depressed and helpless. At eleven o'clock in the morning, the doorbell suddenly rang, and Wigan appeared at my doorstep, which truly overjoyed me. She reached out her warm hands and carefully and considerately helped me into her car. I was so moved that my eyes filled with tears, not knowing how to express my gratitude to her. After school, Wigan drove me back home and told me not to worry about going to school in the future as she would always help me. She laughed and joked, "I am now your personal chauffeur."

On another occasion, the person knocking on the door was not Wigan, but a substitute teacher who came to pick me up. The substitute teacher told me with a smile, "Wigan's mother had surgery, so she couldn't come to class. But she specifically instructed me that there is a student in class who needs to be picked up and dropped off, and that I must not forget her. No student can be left out, and not a single one can be allowed to fall behind."

At the graduation ceremony, I bought a bouquet of fresh flowers and a beautiful shawl. I draped the shawl over Wigan's shoulders with both hands, handed the flowers to her, and bowed in gratitude. When our eyes met, they shone with a beautiful radiance.
