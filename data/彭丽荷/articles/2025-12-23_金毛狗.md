# 金毛狗

**Date:** 2025-12-23 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121252/9208875?zh-cn

起初听人说起[**印度**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/印度)神庙，脑海中浮现出雄伟庙宇与金身神像的画面，如同我曾多次拜访的杭州灵隐寺，庄严宏大，香烟氤氲，令人心生虔敬，但[**新泽西州**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/新泽西州)的BAPS斯瓦米纳拉扬寺庙（BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir）却是另一番景象。

这座由大理石建成的建筑通体雪白，线条流利，既巍峨庄严，又纯净空灵，外观更类似欧洲的大教堂，被誉为大理石奇观。

一个夏日，我们驱车来到此处。一下车，映入眼帘的是粉色围墙和牌楼，随即是一个碧水微漾的水池，池里几束小喷泉轻轻地溅起水花。环绕水池的是绿植和红砖小径，悠扬的梵音在天地间回荡，营造出一种静心的氛围。在欢迎中心，有工作人员提醒游客注意服装，要求上衣有袖、裤裙过膝。

通向主庙的广场两侧是粉色的回廊，廊柱上雕刻着手持不同乐器的天人乐伎，形态各异，神态生动。主庙尖顶高耸入云，在阳光下显得洁白耀眼，气势恢宏，宛如一座石之殿堂。

进入殿堂需脱鞋。我脱下凉鞋，赤脚踏上大理石地面，冰凉的触感让我一时不太习惯。管理人员微笑着解释：赤足更能感受寺庙的神圣与能量。我试着走了几步，最终还是折返回车上穿了袜子，这一来一回耗去了半个多小时。

主庙内禁止拍照，这份限制却让心境更为沉静。安静的氛围、柔和的光影与精美的雕刻完美融合，创造出一种超脱尘世的体验。除了神像，廊柱与石墙上的精致浮雕也引人入胜。

这些浮雕工艺精湛，场面繁复，人物姿态自然传神，无声地讲述着过往圣贤的故事。头顶上，天花板的莲花式雕刻层层盛开，而中央的穹顶则最为壮观，那层层交叠的几何结构，仿佛是宇宙秩序在空中凝结。

这座现代宏伟建筑，由数千块在印度预制雕刻的大理石，运至美国后组装而成。主殿与副殿的神像，共同构筑了斯瓦米纳拉扬（Swaminarayan）教派丰富的神圣世界，而至高无上的主神便是斯瓦米纳拉扬。这位圣者在少年时期便展开长达七年、超过一万两千公里的苦行与朝圣之旅，最终在印度西部奠定了这一宗教传统。

这段历史不禁让我联想到赫尔曼·黑塞笔下的「悉达多」，想到释迦牟尼佛的故事。虽然斯瓦米纳拉扬与释迦牟尼相隔两千多年，一个奠立佛教，一个继承并改革了印度教毗湿奴派的传统，他们都在青少年时期毅然舍弃世俗生活，走上修行之路，最终成为大规模宗教的奠基者。他们都强调戒律与道德，提倡众生平等。他们的生命轨迹，跨越时空，散发着相似的皎皎光辉。

尽管这座雪白的大理石殿堂给人以欧洲教堂的典雅之感，但亚洲寺庙的特色还是比比皆是。主庙前两侧，数头金色大象昂然伫立；入口广场上，一尊少年金身神像讲述着修行故事，神像旁的香案更让人感受到与[**华人**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/华人)寺庙文化的共鸣。

这座寺庙不仅是宗教殿堂，也是文化的交流中心，来自各地的游客络绎不绝，或驻足凝望，或低声祈祷。出来时，我们在烧香台旁驻足，尽管对印度教所知甚少，但仍旧点燃香火。随着那袅袅轻烟升腾，一种宁静悠远的氛围弥漫开来。这场旅程，从视觉的震撼，最终归于心灵的安宁。

## Golden Retriever
Initially, when I heard people talk about Indian temples, images of majestic shrines and golden statues would float into my mind, much like Hangzhou's Lingyin Temple which I have visited many times—solemn, grand, filled with curling incense, inspiring deep reverence. However, the BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir in New Jersey presented a completely different scene.

Built entirely of marble, this structure is snow-white with fluid lines. It is both majestic and solemn, yet pure and ethereal, with an exterior resembling a European cathedral, earning it the reputation of a marble marvel.

One summer day, we drove to this place. As soon as we got out of the car, we were greeted by pink perimeter walls and an archway, followed by a pool of gently rippling clear water, where several small fountains softly splashed. Surrounding the pool were green plants and red-brick paths, with melodious Sanskrit chanting echoing between heaven and earth, creating a serene, meditative atmosphere. At the welcome center, staff reminded visitors to dress appropriately, requiring sleeved tops and pants or skirts that cover the knees.

The corridors leading to the main temple plaza are lined with pink colonnades, their pillars carved with celestial musicians holding different instruments, each unique in form and vivid in expression. The towering spires of the main temple pierce the clouds, shining dazzlingly white in the sunlight, grand and magnificent, like a palace carved of stone.

Entering the hall requires removing one's shoes. I took off my sandals and stepped barefoot onto the marble floor; the icy sensation took me a moment to get used to. A staff member explained with a smile that going barefoot allows one to better feel the temple's sanctity and energy. I tried taking a few steps but ultimately turned back to my car to put on socks, a round trip that took over half an hour.

Photography is prohibited inside the main temple, a restriction that actually made my state of mind more tranquil. The quiet atmosphere, soft light and shadows, and exquisite carvings blended perfectly to create an experience that felt detached from the mortal world. Beyond the statues, the delicate reliefs on the pillars and stone walls were captivating.

These reliefs exhibit exquisite craftsmanship and complex scenes, with natural and lifelike human figures silently telling the stories of past sages. Overhead, the lotus carvings on the ceiling bloom layer by layer, while the central dome is the most spectacular, its overlapping geometric structures seeming like the order of the universe frozen in mid-air.

This modern, grand masterpiece was assembled in the United States from thousands of marble pieces pre-carved in India. The deities in the main and side halls together construct the rich, sacred world of the Swaminarayan sect, with the supreme deity being Swaminarayan himself. This holy figure embarked on a seven-year ascetic and pilgrimage journey spanning over 12,000 kilometers during his youth, ultimately establishing this religious tradition in western India.

This history inevitably reminded me of Hermann Hesse's 'Siddhartha' and the story of Gautama Buddha. Although Swaminarayan and Shakyamuni Buddha lived more than two thousand years apart—one founding Buddhism, the other inheriting and reforming the Vaishnavism tradition of Hinduism—both resolutely abandoned secular life in their youth to embark on a path of spiritual practice, eventually becoming the founders of major religions. Both emphasized discipline and morality and advocated for the equality of all living beings. Their life paths, transcending time and space, radiate a similar brilliant luster.

Although this snow-white marble temple conveys the elegance of a European cathedral, characteristics of Asian temples are still found everywhere. In front of the main temple, several golden elephants stand proudly on both sides; in the entrance plaza, a golden statue of the youth tells the story of his spiritual practice, and the incense burner next to the statue makes one feel an even deeper resonance with Chinese temple culture.

This temple is not only a religious sanctuary but also a center for cultural exchange, drawing a continuous stream of visitors from all over, who either pause to gaze or whisper prayers. As we left, we paused by the incense-burning altar; though we knew very little about Hinduism, we still lit some incense. As the curling smoke drifted upward, a serene and distant atmosphere spread around us. This journey, starting with visual awe, ultimately returned to peace of mind.
