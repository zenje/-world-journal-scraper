# 找女友(下)

**Date:** 2025-10-19 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121251/9064901?zh-cn

玲达每天抽出时间，坐在聪儿的床边耐心地与他说话，她买了不同颜色的气球，把它们飘在聪儿的眼前，告诉他这个气球是红的、那个是绿的、这是黄的，一次又一次不厌其烦地反复。又画了太阳、月亮、星星的图片，对着聪儿一遍又一遍指给他看。

一天，玲达温柔大声说道「这气球是红的」，聪儿的眼睛跟随着妈妈的声音，看着那个红的气球。妈妈又叫着「这个是绿的」，聪儿双眼慢慢地转向了绿色气球。这下子，玲达高兴地几乎跳了起来，孩子有反应、有意识了！

从此以后，玲达买了很多玩具，什么小汽车、毛绒绒的小狗、小猫、小熊等放在聪儿面前，分别告诉他这些玩具叫什么名字。一个月以后，玲达问哪个是小狗？聪儿用手指向了小狗，哪个是小汽车？聪儿都答对了。在以后的日子，任何事情只要玲达讲一遍，聪儿就记住了。在玲达的耐心、执着、坚持不懈努力下，聪儿逐渐恢复正常。

一晃上小学了，聪儿的思维和接受能力比同班小朋友要慢一拍，玲达总会鼓励、表扬他，每天把老师上的课重新讲一遍给聪儿听，让他能跟上进度。

到了中学，聪儿的脑子和思维开窍了，成绩在班上逐渐领先，并获得很多学习奖状。聪儿用优越的成绩考上了上海同济大学电脑专业，玲达笑得合不拢嘴。

聪儿毕业后得到一份很理想又稳定的工作，玲达对儿子说：「你现在样样都称心，年龄不小了，可以考虑找个女朋友。」她向儿子提出了两个要求，从中选一：如果不找女朋友，那就去考美国[**哈佛大学**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/哈佛大学)的研究所。聪儿道：「找女朋友，我没有本事。但我愿意去考研究所，我觉得那比找女朋友要容易得多。」

夏天，一封来自哈佛大学的信函送到了他们家，聪儿正式被录取，拿到攻读硕士学位的通知书，母子二人兴奋不已，玲达更感到无限的光荣和骄傲。

电视台采访了玲达，问她如何把一个病得如此严重的孩子培养成一名优秀的国际名校研究生？同时，还让玲达去电视台现身说法。玲达在演讲过程中，流下无数次的辛酸眼泪，同时眼神又充满了自豪。台下的听众为她鼓掌、喝彩，不少优秀的姑娘一边听，一边手拿餐巾纸不时地拭泪，感动得像个泪孩。

演讲结束后，很多姑娘都向玲达要了她家的地址和与聪儿的联系方式。哇，这下不用发愁了，优秀的姑娘一大堆，看哪个幸运的姑娘能做聪儿的女朋友。

## Finding a Girlfriend (Part II)
Every day, Linda made time to sit by Cong'er's bedside and patiently talk to him. She bought balloons of different colors and floated them in front of his eyes, telling him that this balloon was red, that one was green, and this one was yellow, repeating herself tirelessly time and again. She also drew pictures of the sun, moon, and stars, pointing to them for Cong'er over and over.

One day, Linda said gently but loudly, "This balloon is red." Cong'er's eyes followed his mother's voice and looked at the red balloon. His mother called out again, "This one is green," and Cong'er's eyes slowly turned toward the green balloon. At this, Linda almost jumped with joy—her child had a reaction; he was conscious!

From then on, Linda bought many toys—toy cars, fluffy puppies, kittens, little bears, and so on—and placed them in front of Cong'er, telling him the name of each toy. A month later, Linda asked which one was the puppy, and Cong'er pointed to the puppy. Which one was the toy car? Cong'er got them all right. In the days that followed, as long as Linda explained something once, Cong'er would remember it. Under Linda's patience, dedication, and unremitting efforts, Cong'er gradually returned to normal.

In the blink of an eye, Cong'er was starting elementary school. His cognitive and comprehension abilities were a step slower than those of his classmates. Linda always encouraged and praised him, and every day she would re-teach him the lessons taught by the teacher to help him keep up.

By junior high school, Cong'er's mind and thinking finally clicked. His grades gradually took the lead in class, and he won many academic awards. With outstanding grades, Cong'er was admitted to the computer science department at Tongji University in Shanghai, leaving Linda beaming with joy from ear to ear.

After graduation, Cong'er got a highly desirable and stable job. Linda said to her son, "Everything is going well for you now, and you're not getting any younger. You should consider finding a girlfriend." She gave him two options, asking him to choose one: if he didn't look for a girlfriend, he would have to apply to graduate school at Harvard University in the United States. Cong'er said, "I don't have the knack for finding a girlfriend. But I am willing to apply to graduate school. I think that is much easier than finding a girlfriend."

In the summer, a letter from Harvard University arrived at their home. Cong'er was officially admitted and received his acceptance letter to pursue a master's degree. The mother and son were thrilled, and Linda felt an immense sense of honor and pride.

A television station interviewed Linda, asking her how she raised a child who had been so severely ill into an outstanding graduate student at a world-class university. They also invited her to share her personal experience on TV. During her speech, Linda shed countless bitter tears, but her eyes were filled with pride. The audience applauded and cheered for her. Many excellent young women wiped away tears with tissues as they listened, deeply moved to tears.

After the speech, many young women asked Linda for their home address and Cong'er's contact details. Wow, there was no need to worry now—there was a whole crowd of wonderful girls. It remained to be seen which lucky girl would become Cong'er's girlfriend.
