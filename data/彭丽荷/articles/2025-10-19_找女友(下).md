## English Translation
Finding a Girlfriend (Part 2)

Date: October 19, 2025, 02:00 AM ET
Author: Peng Lihe

Every day, Linda carved out time to sit by Cong'er's bedside and talk to him patiently. She bought balloons of different colors, let them float before his eyes, and tirelessly repeated to him over and over again: this balloon is red, that one is green, and this one is yellow. She also drew pictures of the sun, moon, and stars, pointing to them and showing them to Cong'er time after time.

One day, Linda said softly but clearly, "This balloon is red." Cong'er's eyes followed his mother's voice, looking at the red balloon. His mother then called out, "This one is green," and Cong'er's eyes slowly turned toward the green balloon. At this, Linda was so thrilled she almost jumped for joy—her child was responding; he was conscious!

From then on, Linda bought many toys—toy cars, stuffed dogs, cats, and bears—placed them in front of Cong'er, and told him the name of each toy. A month later, when Linda asked, "Which one is the little dog?" Cong'er pointed to the dog. "Which one is the toy car?" Cong'er got them all right. From that day forward, Cong'er could remember anything after Linda explained it just once. Under Linda’s patient, determined, and unremitting efforts, Cong'er gradually recovered.

In the blink of an eye, he started primary school. Cong'er's thinking and comprehension skills were a step slower than his classmates', but Linda always encouraged and praised him, re-teaching him the school lessons every day so he could keep up.

By the time he reached middle school, Cong'er's mind suddenly opened up. His grades gradually took the lead in class, and he won many academic awards. Cong'er was admitted to Shanghai's Tongji University to major in Computer Science with outstanding grades, leaving Linda beaming with joy.

After graduation, Cong'er secured a highly desirable and stable job. Linda said to him, "Everything is going well for you now, and you're not getting any younger. You should think about finding a girlfriend." She gave her son two options to choose from: if he didn't find a girlfriend, he had to apply for graduate school at Harvard University in the United States. Cong'er replied, "I don't have what it takes to find a girlfriend. But I'm willing to take the exams for graduate school. I think that's much easier than finding a girlfriend."

In the summer, a letter from Harvard University arrived at their home. Cong'er was officially accepted and received his admission notice for a master's degree. Mother and son were incredibly excited, and Linda felt boundless honor and pride.

A TV station interviewed Linda, asking her how she had raised a child who had once been so gravely ill into an outstanding graduate student at a world-renowned university. They also invited her to share her story on-air. During her speech, Linda shed countless tears over her past hardships, yet her eyes were filled with pride. The audience applauded and cheered for her. Many outstanding young women wiped away tears with tissues as they listened, deeply moved.

After the speech, many girls asked Linda for her address and Cong'er's contact information. Wow! Now there was no need to worry—there was a whole crowd of wonderful girls, and it was just a matter of seeing which lucky girl would become Cong'er's girlfriend.
