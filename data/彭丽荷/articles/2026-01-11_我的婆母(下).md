# 我的婆母(下)

**Date:** 2026-01-11 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121251/9243746?zh-cn

十八岁的婆婆进了程家大院，两个贴身的丫头和老干一同陪嫁过去。多善也想跟着姑姑到程家，但没得到批准。

婆婆从此被人称为九奶，她一连生了四个姑娘，觉得自己好没面子，没有给九少爷增光添彩，又担心将来没有继承人，时常闷闷不乐。不久，婆婆又怀上了，她赶紧去寺庙烧香拜佛，祈求菩萨赐个小男孩。

在回家的路上，只听到后面有个妇女指着自己说：「前面这个太太，看样子怀的是个男孩。」婆婆闻到此言，喜出望外，赶忙回过头，堆满笑容问：「大姐，请教您怎么知道我怀的是男孩？」妇女也笑着说：「您看，您虽怀孕，从后面看，您的屁股没有一点凸出来，走路不摇不摆；从前面瞧，肚子是朝前坠，挤在前面一坨，这两点大多数证明是男孩。」

婆婆听后，朝那妇人拱手作揖，热情道谢：「借您的金口，托您的福，谢谢啦。」婆婆立即吩咐老干，叫人挑一担榖子送到妇人家中，表示对其良言的感激。

果真不假，腊月二十几，快要过年时，婆婆生了一个白胖的大儿子，节日和九房得子双重喜庆的气氛，让家中充满了阳光四射的活力，鞭炮齐鸣，锣鼓喧天，好不热闹。

时间很快过去，程家的老爷和老太太都相继离世，大房、二房提出了要分家，九房一直跟着哥嫂过日子，要分家产，九少爷没有心计，什么也不懂，只由得大房、二房摆布。最终婆婆家得到了四、五顷田地，他们还说是照顾九房，多算点给他，实质上这田地是在低漥处最差的位子，每年都会被洪水淹没，收成也很少。婆婆一门心思都在儿子身上，也不会打理财产和家务，就随别人的决定。

分家之后，婆婆也试图当个好主妇。她走进厨房想看看如何做菜，厨师和婆子们笑着说：「九奶，想吃什么，只管吩咐，何必亲自来厨房，这里油烟麻罩的，不要弄脏了九奶的衣服，赶快请回吧。」婆婆就依从他们的话，回到堂屋。

解放后，分田地、斗地主，九少爷戴上了「地主」这桂冠，被发配劳动改造。婆婆吓得没有主张，带着孩子们逃回了娘家，娘家的母亲也逃不脱「无产阶级专政」，被要求思想改造、脱胎换骨、重新做人。六、七十岁的老母亲跟随一批「地富反坏右」一起上山敲石头，改造不到一星期就病倒了。

婆婆母亲的干孙子多善是根红苗正的贫下中农，当上了干部，成了村里的大队长。多善人还正直，不忘旧情，知恩图报，晓得「姑姑」回娘家，干奶奶改造病倒，在政策的允许范围下，安排了「姑姑」的孩子们到省城里读书，并给予婆婆一大家子极大的照顾。婆婆也怕为难多善，提出代母出征，但婆婆哪是干粗活的人？第一榔头下去，就把左手中指给敲弯了，从此婆婆就免去劳动。这都要感谢多善暗中的帮助。

婆婆的女儿和儿子完成学业以后，有了收入，两个女儿和儿子每月各寄给婆婆十元人民币生活费，另一个女儿寄五元，在那个年代有三十五元就是很富裕的家庭。

婆婆和她母亲两人生活过得有滋有味、有板有眼的，多余的钱就悄悄贴补一点给多善的孩子用。这样的生活过了十多年，婆婆的老母亲年迈虚弱，一天躺在婆婆的怀中安详离世。

我们把婆婆从安徽接到上海和我们团聚，一家五口挤在一间二十平方米的房子里。婆婆为人忠厚老实，待人真诚，我们和睦相处，没有争吵。婆婆常夸奖我说：「上海人有教养、有文化。我儿子相中了你，是他的造化。」

婆婆对我像亲生闺女一般，她帮我们带孩子，还拿出女儿寄给她的钱为孙子买奶粉，时常去菜市场买些鸡鱼肉蛋给我们改善伙食。孙子们问她要零花钱，她迫不及待地掏出一把，问这些够吗？孙子们都喜欢她、爱戴她。

婆婆和我们生活了八年，想回老家了，以后可以安葬在母亲的身边。她离开我们家时，我拿了三百元缝在她的棉袄内，关照她有急事时才能拿出来花。

一转眼，婆婆离开我们三十三年，每当想到她老人家，心中泛起了缅怀深情。婆婆呀，如有来世，我俩婆媳再度续缘。（下）

## My Mother-in-Law (Part II)
At eighteen, my mother-in-law entered the Cheng family compound, accompanied by her two personal maidservants and Lao Gan as part of her dowry. Duoshan also wanted to follow his aunt to the Cheng family, but did not receive permission.

From then on, my mother-in-law was known as Ninth Mistress. She gave birth to four girls in a row and felt she had lost face, failing to bring honor to the Ninth Young Master. She also worried about having no heir for the future and was often depressed. Before long, she became pregnant again. She hurriedly went to the temple to burn incense and pray to the Buddha, begging the Bodhisattva to grant her a baby boy.

On her way home, she heard a woman behind her pointing at her and saying, "That lady in front looks like she is carrying a boy." Hearing this, my mother-in-law was overjoyed. She quickly turned around, wearing a broad smile, and asked, "Eldest Sister, how do you know I am carrying a boy?" The woman also smiled and said, "Look, although you are pregnant, from behind, your buttocks do not protrude at all, and your gait is steady. From the front, your belly hangs forward, bunched up in the front. These two signs usually prove it's a boy."

Upon hearing this, my mother-in-law bowed and clasped her hands in gratitude, thanking her warmly, "May your auspicious words come true. Thank you so much." She immediately instructed Lao Gan to have someone deliver a load of grain to the woman's home to express her gratitude for her kind words.

And true to those words, in late December of the lunar calendar, just before the Lunar New Year, my mother-in-law gave birth to a plump, fair baby boy. The double celebration of the holiday and the birth of a son to the Ninth Branch filled the house with radiant vitality. Firecrackers crackled, and drums and gongs resounded, making it exceptionally lively.

Time flew by, and the old master and mistress of the Cheng family passed away one after another. The First and Second Branches proposed dividing the family property. The Ninth Branch had always lived with the older brothers and sisters-in-law; when it came to dividing the estate, the Ninth Young Master had no scheming mind and knew nothing, leaving himself completely at the mercy of the First and Second Branches. In the end, my mother-in-law's family received four to five qing (around 70 to 85 acres) of farmland. They claimed they were looking out for the Ninth Branch by allocating extra, but in reality, this land was in the lowest-lying, worst location, flooded by water every year, yielding very poor harvests. My mother-in-law’s mind was entirely focused on her son, and she knew nothing about managing property or household affairs, so she simply went along with whatever others decided.

After the family division, my mother-in-law also tried to be a good housewife. She walked into the kitchen wanting to see how dishes were cooked, but the chefs and maids smiled and said, "Ninth Mistress, just tell us whatever you want to eat. Why come to the kitchen yourself? It is full of grease and smoke here. Don't get your clothes dirty; please go back." My mother-in-law followed their advice and returned to the main hall.

After Liberation, land was redistributed and landlords were denounced. The Ninth Young Master was labeled a "landlord" and sent away for reform through labor. Terrified and helpless, my mother-in-law took the children and fled back to her maternal home. Her mother there could not escape the "dictatorship of the proletariat" either; she was ordered to undergo ideological reform, to transform thoroughly and become a new person. Her mother, already in her late sixties or seventies, was sent up the mountain to break rocks alongside a group of "landlords, rich peasants, counter-revolutionaries, bad elements, and rightists." She fell ill after less than a week of labor.

Duoshan, the god-grandson of my mother-in-law's mother, was a "red and upright" poor peasant who had become a cadre and the village team leader. He was an upright man who did not forget old favors and believed in repaying kindness. Knowing his "aunt" had returned to her maternal home and his god-grandmother had fallen ill from labor reform, he worked within the boundaries of policy to arrange for his "aunt's" children to study in the provincial capital, while providing immense care to my mother-in-law's entire family. My mother-in-law, fearing she might make things difficult for Duoshan, offered to take her mother's place in labor. But how could my mother-in-law handle heavy physical labor? With her very first hammer strike, she bent her left middle finger. From then on, she was exempted from labor. This was all thanks to Duoshan's secret assistance.

After my mother-in-law's daughters and son finished their studies, they had incomes. Two daughters and the son each sent her ten yuan a month for living expenses, while another daughter sent five yuan. In those days, thirty-five yuan made for a very wealthy household.

My mother-in-law and her mother lived a structured, colorful, and comfortable life. They would quietly use any spare money to support Duoshan’s children. This kind of life went on for over ten years until my mother-in-law’s elderly mother, frail with age, passed away peacefully one day in her arms.

We brought my mother-in-law from Anhui to Shanghai to reunite with us, and our family of five squeezed into a twenty-square-meter room. My mother-in-law was honest, kind, and sincere, and we lived together in harmony without any arguments. She often praised me, saying, "People from Shanghai are well-mannered and educated. It was my son's blessing that he chose you."

My mother-in-law treated me like her own daughter. She helped us raise the children and even used the money her daughters sent her to buy milk powder for her grandson. She would often go to the market to buy chicken, fish, meat, and eggs to improve our meals. When her grandchildren asked for pocket money, she would eagerly pull out a handful and ask if it was enough. The grandchildren all liked and loved her deeply.

After living with us for eight years, she wanted to return to her hometown so that she could eventually be buried beside her mother. When she left our home, I took three hundred yuan and sewed it inside her padded jacket, telling her to save it only for emergencies.

In the blink of an eye, thirty-three years have passed since my mother-in-law left us. Whenever I think of her, deep feelings of remembrance swell in my heart. Oh, Mother-in-law, if there is a next life, may we be mother and daughter-in-law once again. (Part II)
