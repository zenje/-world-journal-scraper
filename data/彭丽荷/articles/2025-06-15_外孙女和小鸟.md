# 外孙女和小鸟

**Date:** 2025-06-15 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷

一天，外孙女囡囡的爸爸下班后，特地从[**圣地牙哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego）的北边赶到南面的老镇交换市场（Old Town Swap Meet），选购了一只绿色羽毛的鸟。这只鸟的双爪紧紧地攥住鸟笼中央悬挂的一根小木棍上，用陌生而又紧张的神态来到我们家。

女婿对五岁的囡囡说：「这只小鸟是送给妳的生日礼物，喜欢吗？」囡囡看着小鸟，脸上展开笑容，拍着双手，嘴里叫着「小鸟、小鸟我爱你」。她把头紧紧贴在笼子上，做出要和小鸟拥抱和亲吻的动作，小鸟并不知道囡囡这亲暱的模样是因为喜欢牠，反而吓了一跳，在笼子里乱飞、乱跳，似乎挣扎着要飞出笼子。这下子，囡囡也被吓得不轻，原本笑嘻嘻的小脸蛋马上挂上泪珠，哇哇地大哭起来。

当囡囡上学时，她对爸爸说：「我去学校了，小鸟一个人很寂寞，我们再去买一只，二只小鸟可以热闹一些。」 女婿见囡囡如此喜欢，也就答应她的要求。

第两只鸟的羽毛是蓝色带有黑、白小点，很漂亮。两只鸟住在同一间笼子里，显得很拥挤，为了让牠们有足够的活动空间，又换了个大笼子。囡囡觉得两只鸟有时搞不清楚，就给牠们取了名字：原来的那只叫Ellie，新的这只唤Eddie。每次女儿给囡囡买玩具时，囡囡总忘不了也要给Ellie和Eddie买，她选购了小圆镜子、小挂球、小响铃，用绳子绑好挂在笼子的顶端，后来还买了一个小梯子，让Ellie、Eddie学着上下楼梯，挺滑稽的。

前阵子，囡囡发现Ellie无精打采，老是闭着眼睛想睡觉，女婿想着要带牠去看兽医，没想到第二天清晨掀开鸟笼上的布罩时，发现Ellie不声不响地倒在笼子里，一动也不动。

早餐时，女婿把这个坏消息告诉了囡囡，她一听，泪珠儿像断了线似地滚落在胸前，早饭吃不下，上课时也无法集中精神，一心悼念着她心爱的Ellie。

不久，女婿又到市场拎回一只浅黄色羽毛的小鸟，牠一进笼子就欢快地和蓝色羽毛的Eddie亲暱起来，囡囡为家中的新伙伴取名为Eggie，又快乐了起来。

## English Translation
One day, after my granddaughter Nannan's father got off work, he specially traveled from the north of San Diego to the Old Town Swap Meet in the south to buy a green-feathered bird. With its claws tightly gripping a small wooden stick hanging in the center of the cage, the bird arrived at our home with an unfamiliar and anxious look.

My son-in-law said to five-year-old Nannan, 'This little bird is a birthday gift for you. Do you like it?' Looking at the bird, Nannan broke into a smile, clapped her hands, and cried out, 'Little bird, little bird, I love you!' She pressed her head tightly against the cage, attempting to hug and kiss the bird. Not knowing that Nannan’s affectionate gesture was because she liked it, the bird was startled instead. It fluttered and hopped about frantically, seemingly struggling to escape the cage. This frightened Nannan quite a bit too. Her smiling little face instantly filled with tears, and she began to sob loudly.

Later, after my son-in-law finished cleaning the cage, Nannan busied herself feeding the bird and giving it water. She even told the bird, 'Daddy makes me eat vegetables every day, and you can’t go without them either.' As she spoke, she took some spinach leaves and poked them through the gaps in the cage, which the bird happily accepted. Sometimes, Nannan would hold some millet in her palm and stretch her small hand into the cage to feed it. The bird wasn't afraid either; as if knowing she liked it, it would hop onto her palm and eat joyfully. The bird was very smart. Whenever Nannan walked by the cage, it would chirp non-stop as if saying hello. Sometimes when Nannan sang, the bird would dance along.

When Nannan was leaving for school, she said to her father, 'I’m going to school now. The little bird will be so lonely all by itself. Let’s buy another one so they can keep each other company.' Seeing how much Nannan loved it, my son-in-law agreed to her request.

The second bird had beautiful blue feathers with small black and white spots. Having both birds in the same cage felt crowded, so to give them enough room to move around, they were moved to a larger cage. Since Nannan sometimes got them mixed up, she gave them names: the original one was Ellie, and the new one was called Eddie. Every time my daughter bought toys for Nannan, Nannan never forgot to get some for Ellie and Eddie too. She picked out a small round mirror, a little hanging ball, and a small bell, which she tied with string and hung from the top of the cage. Later, she even bought a small ladder for Ellie and Eddie to learn how to climb up and down, which was quite amusing.

A while ago, Nannan noticed that Ellie was listless, always keeping her eyes closed as if wanting to sleep. My son-in-law thought about taking her to the vet, but to his surprise, when he lifted the cloth cover off the cage the next morning, he found Ellie lying silently and motionlessly inside.

During breakfast, my son-in-law broke the sad news to Nannan. Upon hearing it, her tears streamed down like broken strings. She couldn't eat her breakfast and couldn't concentrate in class, deeply mourning her beloved Ellie.

Before long, my son-in-law brought home another little bird with light yellow feathers from the market. As soon as it entered the cage, it happily bonded with the blue-feathered Eddie. Nannan named their new companion Eggie and became happy once again.
