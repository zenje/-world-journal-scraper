# 外孙女和小鸟

**Date:** 2025-06-15 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷

一天，外孙女囡囡的爸爸下班后，特地从[**圣地牙哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego）的北边赶到南面的老镇交换市场（Old Town Swap Meet），选购了一只绿色羽毛的鸟。这只鸟的双爪紧紧地攥住鸟笼中央悬挂的一根小木棍上，用陌生而又紧张的神态来到我们家。

女婿对五岁的囡囡说：「这只小鸟是送给妳的生日礼物，喜欢吗？」囡囡看着小鸟，脸上展开笑容，拍着双手，嘴里叫着「小鸟、小鸟我爱你」。她把头紧紧贴在笼子上，做出要和小鸟拥抱和亲吻的动作，小鸟并不知道囡囡这亲暱的模样是因为喜欢牠，反而吓了一跳，在笼子里乱飞、乱跳，似乎挣扎着要飞出笼子。这下子，囡囡也被吓得不轻，原本笑嘻嘻的小脸蛋马上挂上泪珠，哇哇地大哭起来。

后来女婿清洁完鸟笼，囡囡忙着给小鸟添食加水，还对小鸟说：「爸爸每天都叫我吃蔬菜，你也不可以缺少呀。」说着说着，就去拿了一些菠菜叶子，放在鸟笼的缝隙中，小鸟也愉快地接受了。囡囡有时拿些[**小米**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/小米)托在手掌上，把小手伸进笼子喂食，小鸟也不害怕，似乎是知道囡囡喜欢牠，就顺势跳到囡囡手掌上，欢乐地吃饭。小鸟很有灵性，只要囡囡经过笼子，小鸟就会叽叽喳喳叫个不停，像是在打招呼；囡囡有时唱歌，小鸟也会随之起舞。

当囡囡上学时，她对爸爸说：「我去学校了，小鸟一个人很寂寞，我们再去买一只，二只小鸟可以热闹一些。」 女婿见囡囡如此喜欢，也就答应她的要求。

第两只鸟的羽毛是蓝色带有黑、白小点，很漂亮。两只鸟住在同一间笼子里，显得很拥挤，为了让牠们有足够的活动空间，又换了个大笼子。囡囡觉得两只鸟有时搞不清楚，就给牠们取了名字：原来的那只叫Ellie，新的这只唤Eddie。每次女儿给囡囡买玩具时，囡囡总忘不了也要给Ellie和Eddie买，她选购了小圆镜子、小挂球、小响铃，用绳子绑好挂在笼子的顶端，后来还买了一个小梯子，让Ellie、Eddie学着上下楼梯，挺滑稽的。

前阵子，囡囡发现Ellie无精打采，老是闭着眼睛想睡觉，女婿想着要带牠去看兽医，没想到第二天清晨掀开鸟笼上的布罩时，发现Ellie不声不响地倒在笼子里，一动也不动。

早餐时，女婿把这个坏消息告诉了囡囡，她一听，泪珠儿像断了线似地滚落在胸前，早饭吃不下，上课时也无法集中精神，一心悼念着她心爱的Ellie。

不久，女婿又到市场拎回一只浅黄色羽毛的小鸟，牠一进笼子就欢快地和蓝色羽毛的Eddie亲暱起来，囡囡为家中的新伙伴取名为Eggie，又快乐了起来。

## English Translation
One day, after getting off work, my granddaughter Nannan's father made a special trip from the northern part of San Diego to the Old Town Swap Meet in the south to pick out a green-feathered bird. With its claws tightly gripping a small wooden perch suspended in the center of the cage, the bird arrived at our home with an unfamiliar and anxious demeanor.

My son-in-law said to five-year-old Nannan, "This little bird is a birthday present for you. Do you like it?" Nannan looked at the bird, a smile spreading across her face as she clapped her hands, crying out, "Little bird, little bird, I love you!" She pressed her head tightly against the cage, making a motion as if to hug and kiss the bird. The little bird, not understanding that Nannan's affectionate behavior was out of love, was startled instead. It began fluttering and jumping frantically inside the cage, as if struggling to break free. This gave Nannan quite a scare too; her smiling little face instantly clouded over with tears, and she began to wail.

Later, after my son-in-law finished cleaning the cage, Nannan busied herself with adding food and water for the bird. She even said to it, "Daddy tells me to eat vegetables every day, so you can't go without them either." As she spoke, she went to get some spinach leaves and slipped them through the gaps in the cage, which the little bird happily accepted. Sometimes Nannan would hold some millet in her palm and stretch her little hand into the cage to feed it. The bird wasn't afraid at all; seeming to understand that Nannan liked it, it would hop onto her palm and eat joyfully. The bird was very intelligent and spirited. Whenever Nannan walked past the cage, it would chirp non-stop as if saying hello; and sometimes when Nannan sang, the bird would dance along.

When Nannan was about to go to school, she said to her dad, "I'm going to school now. The little bird will be very lonely by itself. Let's go buy another one so the two birds can keep each other company." Seeing how much Nannan loved it, her father agreed to her request.

The second bird had beautiful blue feathers with tiny black and white spots. Living together in the same cage felt quite crowded, so to give them enough room to move around, they were moved to a larger cage. Because Nannan found it hard to tell the two birds apart sometimes, she gave them names: the original one was called Ellie, and the new one was named Eddie. Every time my daughter bought toys for Nannan, Nannan never forgot to buy some for Ellie and Eddie too. She picked out a small round mirror, hanging balls, and little bells, tying them with string to hang from the top of the cage. Later, she even bought a tiny ladder to let Ellie and Eddie practice climbing up and down, which was quite comical.

Not long ago, Nannan noticed that Ellie was listless, always closing its eyes and wanting to sleep. Her father was planning to take it to the vet, but to their surprise, when he lifted the cloth cover off the cage the next morning, he found Ellie lying motionless and silent inside.

During breakfast, her father broke the sad news to Nannan. Upon hearing it, her tears rolled down her chest like a broken string of pearls. She couldn't eat her breakfast and was unable to concentrate in class, her heart entirely consumed by mourning her beloved Ellie.

Before long, her father brought home another light-yellow feathered bird from the market. As soon as it entered the cage, it playfully bonded with the blue-feathered Eddie. Nannan named their new companion Eggie, and her happiness returned once again.
