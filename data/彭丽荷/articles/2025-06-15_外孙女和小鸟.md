# 外孙女和小鸟

**Date:** 2025-06-15 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121252/8796146?zh-cn

一天，外孙女囡囡的爸爸下班后，特地从[**圣地牙哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego）的北边赶到南面的老镇交换市场（Old Town Swap Meet），选购了一只绿色羽毛的鸟。这只鸟的双爪紧紧地攥住鸟笼中央悬挂的一根小木棍上，用陌生而又紧张的神态来到我们家。

女婿对五岁的囡囡说：「这只小鸟是送给妳的生日礼物，喜欢吗？」囡囡看着小鸟，脸上展开笑容，拍着双手，嘴里叫着「小鸟、小鸟我爱你」。她把头紧紧贴在笼子上，做出要和小鸟拥抱和亲吻的动作，小鸟并不知道囡囡这亲暱的模样是因为喜欢牠，反而吓了一跳，在笼子里乱飞、乱跳，似乎挣扎着要飞出笼子。这下子，囡囡也被吓得不轻，原本笑嘻嘻的小脸蛋马上挂上泪珠，哇哇地大哭起来。

后来女婿清洁完鸟笼，囡囡忙着给小鸟添食加水，还对小鸟说：「爸爸每天都叫我吃蔬菜，你也不可以缺少呀。」说着说着，就去拿了一些菠菜叶子，放在鸟笼的缝隙中，小鸟也愉快地接受了。囡囡有时拿些[**小米**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/小米)托在手掌上，把小手伸进笼子喂食，小鸟也不害怕，似乎是知道囡囡喜欢牠，就顺势跳到囡囡手掌上，欢乐地吃饭。小鸟很有灵性，只要囡囡经过笼子，小鸟就会叽叽喳喳叫个不停，像是在打招呼；囡囡有时唱歌，小鸟也会随之起舞。

当囡囡上学时，她对爸爸说：「我去学校了，小鸟一个人很寂寞，我们再去买一只，二只小鸟可以热闹一些。」 女婿见囡囡如此喜欢，也就答应她的要求。

第两只鸟的羽毛是蓝色带有黑、白小点，很漂亮。两只鸟住在同一间笼子里，显得很拥挤，为了让牠们有足够的活动空间，又换了个大笼子。囡囡觉得两只鸟有时搞不清楚，就给牠们取了名字：原来的那只叫Ellie，新的这只唤Eddie。每次女儿给囡囡买玩具时，囡囡总忘不了也要给Ellie和Eddie买，她选购了小圆镜子、小挂球、小响铃，用绳子绑好挂在笼子的顶端，后来还买了一个小梯子，让Ellie、Eddie学着上下楼梯，挺滑稽的。

前阵子，囡囡发现Ellie无精打采，老是闭着眼睛想睡觉，女婿想着要带牠去看兽医，没想到第二天清晨掀开鸟笼上的布罩时，发现Ellie不声不响地倒在笼子里，一动也不动。

早餐时，女婿把这个坏消息告诉了囡囡，她一听，泪珠儿像断了线似地滚落在胸前，早饭吃不下，上课时也无法集中精神，一心悼念着她心爱的Ellie。

不久，女婿又到市场拎回一只浅黄色羽毛的小鸟，牠一进笼子就欢快地和蓝色羽毛的Eddie亲暱起来，囡囡为家中的新伙伴取名为Eggie，又快乐了起来。

## My Granddaughter and the Little Bird
One day, after my granddaughter Nannan's father got off work, he made a special trip from the north of San Diego to the Old Town Swap Meet in the south to buy a bird with green feathers. This bird's claws tightly clutched a small wooden stick hanging in the center of the cage, and it arrived at our home with an unfamiliar and anxious look.

My son-in-law said to five-year-old Nannan, "This little bird is a birthday gift for you. Do you like it?" Nannan looked at the little bird, a smile spreading across her face. She clapped her hands and cried, "Little bird, little bird, I love you!" She pressed her head tightly against the cage, making motions to hug and kiss the bird. The little bird did not know that Nannan's affectionate actions were because she liked it; instead, it was startled, flying and jumping wildly inside the cage, seemingly struggling to escape. At this, Nannan was also quite frightened; her originally smiling little face was instantly covered in tears, and she burst into loud cries.

Later, after my son-in-law cleaned the cage, Nannan busied herself with adding food and water for the bird. She even said to it, "Dad tells me to eat vegetables every day, and you can't go without them either." As she spoke, she went to get some spinach leaves and placed them in the gaps of the cage, which the bird happily accepted. Sometimes Nannan would hold some millet in her palm and stretch her little hand into the cage to feed it. The bird wasn't afraid; it seemed to know Nannan liked it, so it naturally hopped onto her palm to eat joyfully. The little bird was very intelligent. Whenever Nannan passed by the cage, it would chirp non-stop as if saying hello; sometimes when Nannan sang, the bird would dance along.

When Nannan was going to school, she said to her father, "I'm going to school. The little bird will be lonely by itself. Let's buy another one so the two birds can keep each other company." Seeing how much Nannan loved it, my son-in-law agreed to her request.

The second bird's feathers were blue with small black and white spots, very beautiful. The two birds living in the same cage made it look crowded, so to give them enough space to move around, they were moved into a larger cage. Nannan felt she sometimes couldn't tell the two birds apart, so she named them: the original one was called Ellie, and the new one was named Eddie. Every time my daughter bought toys for Nannan, Nannan never forgot to buy some for Ellie and Eddie too. She picked out a small round mirror, a little hanging ball, and a small bell, tied them with string, and hung them from the top of the cage. Later, she even bought a small ladder to let Ellie and Eddie learn to go up and down, which was quite amusing.

A while ago, Nannan noticed that Ellie was listless, always keeping her eyes closed and wanting to sleep. My son-in-law was thinking about taking her to the vet, but to his surprise, when he lifted the cloth cover off the birdcage the next morning, he found Ellie lying quietly in the cage, completely motionless.

At breakfast, my son-in-law broke the bad news to Nannan. Upon hearing it, her tears rolled down her chest like a broken string of pearls; she couldn't eat her breakfast and couldn't concentrate in class, mourning her beloved Ellie with all her heart.

Not long after, my son-in-law brought home another little bird with light yellow feathers from the market. As soon as it entered the cage, it happily became intimate with the blue-feathered Eddie. Nannan named the new companion Eggie and became happy once again.
