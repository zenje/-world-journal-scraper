# 「丫头」堂哥

**Date:** 2026-02-07 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷

由祖父作主为大伯订了一门婚事。只听说村里的名嘴姚媒婆是顶呱呱的月老牵线人，大伯的婚姻就是姚媒婆牵线搭桥撮合的其中一对新人。

姚媒婆走进了大伯家的庭院，人还没有坐稳，就笑嘻嘻道：「我是来讨杯喜酒的。」当家人送上了茶水和水烟袋，皱巴巴的脸上展现出眉飞舞色、笑逐颜开的神态，满口夸奖女方家庭如何如何好，方圆十里都能知晓赵家的富有，田地、房产、生意有多少多少，并说女孩读过书、受过教育，懂事、能干、聪明……，反正优点报尽，好话说全。

喜娘把头盖红丝巾新娘子的手交给伯父时，对着新郎说：「新娘新娘，从今以后，新娘就是你新的娘，她来照顾你的生活起居的。」此时的新郎喜笑颜开，得意忘形，不知道有多少激动和兴奋。

当伯父把新娘子的红盖头捎开之后，顿时心中一沉，睁大眼睛仔细地看了看，只见新娘从额头到鼻梁处有一道很深的疤痕，疤痕影响了整个脸蛋，即使再漂亮的姑娘也会变丑。伯父一紧张，深深吸了口凉气，他从高温掉到冰库似的，什么激情都没有了。

由于祖父的威严，大伯被迫和伯母圆了房。说来奇怪，这个伯母还真有点本领，慢慢地制服了伯父，伯父见到伯母总是低头哈腰、陪笑说话。以后的三年中，伯母先后生下了两个男孩，这下伯母的腰板子挺得更直了，伯父赚来的工资如数上交，不敢留下一文钱，言听计从。

不知是什么基因，伯父和伯母俩生的两个男孩长得高大、帅气、阳光，高高的鼻梁、漥漥的眼眶中嵌着一双又大又黑有神的眸子，头发微微卷曲，带有点洋气，把祖父和大伯乐坏了，疼爱出奇。不知怎么想出来的，老大取名为「丫头」，说是叫得贱一点好养。

丫头哥高中年代，因帅气、英俊，惹来不少女同学的爱慕，有的女生把「派克」金笔悄悄地放进了丫头哥的铅笔盒里，有的女同学拿着进口的巧克力和奶油糖往他手里塞，更有女孩把自己的零花钱拽进了他的衣袋……。

一九四八年，丫头哥随着一大批学生来到了西南联大，第二年的夏天，西南联大的学生绝大多数随着[**国民党**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/国民党)去了台湾。在这骨节眼上，丫头哥发起了高烧，说是得了「猩红热」，每到傍晚体温就会上升，要烧到摄氏四十度左右，到了白天，温度又下降了。这样反复循环，持续了好几个星期，人也无法站立行走。其他的学生都自顾不暇地打好行装，等候[**机票**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/机票)，纷纷撤离，没有人能顾得上丫头哥。宿舍里的男女学生基本上都走完了，丫头哥只能听天由命，自生自灭。

当丫头哥烧得不省人事，他身边出现了一名女同学，拿了冷毛巾敷在他的前额，忙着帮他熬粥、倒水、拿药的。她叫刘智玲，她决心留下来照顾丫头哥。刘智玲是从浙江到西南联大的，性格善良温柔，只是脸上有好几颗[**大麻**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/大麻)子，影响了她的容貌，她早就注意到丫头哥，无奈自己美中不足，不敢接近，她想用真情实意打动他的心。

经过一个多月的艰辛照料、体贴入微的关心，丫头哥缓慢地恢复，而刘智玲始终陪伴左右，日久生情，丫头哥念她对自己一片真心，就这样两人在一起了。

去台湾的飞机已经停飞，该走的都撤完了。丫头哥和刘智玲两人来到上海，丫头哥找到了一份好差事，刘智玲怀孕生儿子，接着又迎来了女儿，她在家相夫教子，这样的日子真还可以。

丫头哥乘公交车上班，车上的一个卖票员名唤刘贞娴，长得秀丽端庄，声音特别甜美，动人可爱，光鲜照人。而丫头哥英俊潇洒，帅气十足，两人的眼神一下就对上了。丫头哥每天都准时乘上这班车，挺享受这段乘车的时间，不久就把卖票小姐娶回了家。

刘智玲不吵不闹，她也早料到丫头哥总有一天会出格的。丫头哥在小南门租借了紧挨的两间房子，让二个「刘」比邻而居。至于丫头哥，他安排自己每周轮换着去大刘和小刘的住处，二「刘」和睦相处，共度时光。丫头哥想办法把小刘安排到自己工作单位当了文书，数年之后，小刘也产下一子，这个小儿子交给大刘抚养管教，丫头哥的收入全部上交给大刘，大刘把家庭安排得有来倒去，皆大欢喜。

安稳的日子不长，一个一个的运动来了，丫头哥首当其冲受到批判，社会主义国家怎能允许有两个老婆？「地富反坏右」丫头哥属于哪类分子呢？结果坏分子的帽子顶在丫头哥头上。他自己反省，决定离婚，离掉大的还是小的？让他很纠结，于是决定两个都离掉。大小二刘也都无奈，各自带了自己的孩子各奔东西，一家人散伙了。

## English Translation
# Cousin "Yatou"

**Date:** 2026-02-07 01:00 ET  
**Author:** Peng Lihe  

My grandfather took it upon himself to arrange a marriage for my eldest uncle. We had only heard that Matchmaker Yao, the most famous silver tongue in the village, was a stellar matchmaker, and my uncle's marriage was just one of the many unions she successfully brought together. 

Before Matchmaker Yao had even settled into her seat in my uncle's courtyard, she grinned and said, "I'm here to ask for a cup of wedding wine." When the head of the household served her tea and a water pipe, her wrinkled face lit up with joy. She was full of praise, gushing about how wonderful the bride's family was, saying that everyone within a ten-mile radius knew of the Zhao family's wealth—their vast lands, properties, and businesses. She added that the girl was educated, sensible, capable, and smart... In short, she listed every possible merit and spoke nothing but praise.

When the wedding assistant handed the hand of the bride, whose head was covered with a red silk veil, to my uncle, she said to the groom, "Bride, bride, from now on, the bride (*xin-niang*) is your new mother (*xin de niang*). She is here to take care of your daily life." At this, the groom beamed with joy, completely beside himself with excitement.

But when my uncle lifted the bride's red veil, his heart sank instantly. He widened his eyes to take a closer look, only to see a deep scar running from her forehead to the bridge of her nose. The scar ruined her entire face; even the most beautiful girl would have been rendered ugly by it. My uncle gasped in panic. It felt like falling from a hot spring into an icebox, and all his passion instantly vanished.

Due to my grandfather’s authority, my uncle was forced to consummate the marriage with my aunt. Strangely enough, this aunt of mine was actually quite capable, and she gradually brought my uncle to heel. Whenever my uncle saw her, he would bow, scrape, and smile obsequiously. Over the next three years, my aunt gave birth to two boys one after the other. This made her stand even taller. My uncle handed over his wages in full, not daring to keep a single penny, and listened to her every word.

By some stroke of genetic luck, the two boys born to my uncle and aunt grew up to be tall, handsome, and vibrant. With high nose bridges and deep-set eyes framing large, dark, and spirited pupils, their slightly wavy hair gave them an exotic, foreign look. This brought endless joy to my grandfather and uncle, who doted on them extraordinarily. For reasons unknown, the eldest was named "Yatou" (Little Girl), under the belief that giving a child a cheap nickname would make them easier to raise.

During his high school years, Cousin Yatou’s handsome and dashing looks attracted the admiration of many female classmates. Some girls would quietly slip "Parker" fountain pens into his pencil case; others would stuff imported chocolates and cream candies into his hands; and some would even thrust their pocket money into his coat pockets...

In 1948, Cousin Yatou went to National Southwestern Associated University along with a large group of students. In the summer of the following year, the vast majority of the university's students followed the Nationalist Party (KMT) to Taiwan. At this critical juncture, Cousin Yatou came down with a high fever, said to be scarlet fever. Every evening, his temperature would spike to around 40 degrees Celsius, only to drop during the day. This cycle repeated for several weeks, leaving him unable to stand or walk. The other students, preoccupied with packing their bags and waiting for plane tickets to evacuate, could not spare any attention for Cousin Yatou. With almost all the students having left the dormitory, Cousin Yatou was left to his fate, to live or die on his own.

Just when Cousin Yatou was burning hot and unconscious, a female classmate appeared by his side. She placed cold towels on his forehead, and busied herself cooking porridge, pouring water, and fetching medicine for him. Her name was Liu Zhiling. She had resolved to stay behind to care for Cousin Yatou. Liu Zhiling had come to Southwestern Associated University from Zhejiang. She was kind and gentle, but her face was marred by several large pockmarks, which affected her appearance. She had noticed Cousin Yatou long ago, but due to her own physical flaws, she hadn't dared to approach him. She hoped to win his heart with her sincerity and devotion.

After more than a month of painstaking care and meticulous attention, Cousin Yatou slowly recovered. With Liu Zhiling constantly by his side, feelings naturally blossomed over time. Grateful for her unwavering sincerity, Cousin Yatou stayed with her, and they became a couple.

Flights to Taiwan had stopped, and everyone who was supposed to leave had evacuated. Cousin Yatou and Liu Zhiling went to Shanghai, where Cousin Yatou found a good job. Liu Zhiling gave birth to a son and then a daughter. She stayed at home to look after the family and educate the children, and they lived a rather comfortable life.

Cousin Yatou commuted to work by bus. A female conductor on the bus, named Liu Zhenxian, was beautiful, elegant, and had an exceptionally sweet, moving, and charming voice. Cousin Yatou himself was tall, handsome, and full of charm, and their eyes locked instantly. Cousin Yatou began taking this bus on time every day, thoroughly enjoying the commute, and before long, he married the young conductor and brought her home.

Liu Zhiling did not make a scene; she had long anticipated that Cousin Yatou would step out of line one day. Cousin Yatou rented two adjacent rooms in Xiaonanmen, allowing the two "Lius" to live side by side. As for Cousin Yatou, he arranged to take turns staying with the elder Liu and the younger Liu on alternating weeks. The two "Lius" lived together in harmony and shared their time. Cousin Yatou managed to secure a clerical job for the younger Liu at his workplace. A few years later, the younger Liu also gave birth to a son, who was handed over to the elder Liu to raise and discipline. Cousin Yatou handed all his income over to the elder Liu, who managed the household so masterfully that everyone was happy.

But the peaceful days did not last. One political movement after another arrived, and Cousin Yatou was among the first to be targeted for criticism. How could a socialist country tolerate having two wives? Which category of the "Five Black Categories"—landlords, rich peasants, counter-revolutionaries, bad elements, and rightists—did Cousin Yatou belong to? In the end, the label of "bad element" was pinned onto Cousin Yatou. Reflecting on his situation, he decided to get a divorce. But which one should he divorce—the elder or the younger? Torn by this dilemma, he decided to divorce both. Left with no choice, both the elder and younger Lius took their respective children and went their separate ways. The family fell apart.
