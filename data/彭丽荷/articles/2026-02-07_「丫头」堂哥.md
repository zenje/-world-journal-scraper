# 「丫头」堂哥

**Date:** 2026-02-07 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121251/9302884?zh-cn

由祖父作主为大伯订了一门婚事。只听说村里的名嘴姚媒婆是顶呱呱的月老牵线人，大伯的婚姻就是姚媒婆牵线搭桥撮合的其中一对新人。

姚媒婆走进了大伯家的庭院，人还没有坐稳，就笑嘻嘻道：「我是来讨杯喜酒的。」当家人送上了茶水和水烟袋，皱巴巴的脸上展现出眉飞舞色、笑逐颜开的神态，满口夸奖女方家庭如何如何好，方圆十里都能知晓赵家的富有，田地、房产、生意有多少多少，并说女孩读过书、受过教育，懂事、能干、聪明……，反正优点报尽，好话说全。

喜娘把头盖红丝巾新娘子的手交给伯父时，对着新郎说：「新娘新娘，从今以后，新娘就是你新的娘，她来照顾你的生活起居的。」此时的新郎喜笑颜开，得意忘形，不知道有多少激动和兴奋。

当伯父把新娘子的红盖头捎开之后，顿时心中一沉，睁大眼睛仔细地看了看，只见新娘从额头到鼻梁处有一道很深的疤痕，疤痕影响了整个脸蛋，即使再漂亮的姑娘也会变丑。伯父一紧张，深深吸了口凉气，他从高温掉到冰库似的，什么激情都没有了。

由于祖父的威严，大伯被迫和伯母圆了房。说来奇怪，这个伯母还真有点本领，慢慢地制服了伯父，伯父见到伯母总是低头哈腰、陪笑说话。以后的三年中，伯母先后生下了两个男孩，这下伯母的腰板子挺得更直了，伯父赚来的工资如数上交，不敢留下一文钱，言听计从。

不知是什么基因，伯父和伯母俩生的两个男孩长得高大、帅气、阳光，高高的鼻梁、漥漥的眼眶中嵌着一双又大又黑有神的眸子，头发微微卷曲，带有点洋气，把祖父和大伯乐坏了，疼爱出奇。不知怎么想出来的，老大取名为「丫头」，说是叫得贱一点好养。

丫头哥高中年代，因帅气、英俊，惹来不少女同学的爱慕，有的女生把「派克」金笔悄悄地放进了丫头哥的铅笔盒里，有的女同学拿着进口的巧克力和奶油糖往他手里塞，更有女孩把自己的零花钱拽进了他的衣袋……。

一九四八年，丫头哥随着一大批学生来到了西南联大，第二年的夏天，西南联大的学生绝大多数随着[**国民党**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/国民党)去了台湾。在这骨节眼上，丫头哥发起了高烧，说是得了「猩红热」，每到傍晚体温就会上升，要烧到摄氏四十度左右，到了白天，温度又下降了。这样反复循环，持续了好几个星期，人也无法站立行走。其他的学生都自顾不暇地打好行装，等候[**机票**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/机票)，纷纷撤离，没有人能顾得上丫头哥。宿舍里的男女学生基本上都走完了，丫头哥只能听天由命，自生自灭。

当丫头哥烧得不省人事，他身边出现了一名女同学，拿了冷毛巾敷在他的前额，忙着帮他熬粥、倒水、拿药的。她叫刘智玲，她决心留下来照顾丫头哥。刘智玲是从浙江到西南联大的，性格善良温柔，只是脸上有好几颗[**大麻**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/大麻)子，影响了她的容貌，她早就注意到丫头哥，无奈自己美中不足，不敢接近，她想用真情实意打动他的心。

经过一个多月的艰辛照料、体贴入微的关心，丫头哥缓慢地恢复，而刘智玲始终陪伴左右，日久生情，丫头哥念她对自己一片真心，就这样两人在一起了。

去台湾的飞机已经停飞，该走的都撤完了。丫头哥和刘智玲两人来到上海，丫头哥找到了一份好差事，刘智玲怀孕生儿子，接着又迎来了女儿，她在家相夫教子，这样的日子真还可以。

丫头哥乘公交车上班，车上的一个卖票员名唤刘贞娴，长得秀丽端庄，声音特别甜美，动人可爱，光鲜照人。而丫头哥英俊潇洒，帅气十足，两人的眼神一下就对上了。丫头哥每天都准时乘上这班车，挺享受这段乘车的时间，不久就把卖票小姐娶回了家。

刘智玲不吵不闹，她也早料到丫头哥总有一天会出格的。丫头哥在小南门租借了紧挨的两间房子，让二个「刘」比邻而居。至于丫头哥，他安排自己每周轮换着去大刘和小刘的住处，二「刘」和睦相处，共度时光。丫头哥想办法把小刘安排到自己工作单位当了文书，数年之后，小刘也产下一子，这个小儿子交给大刘抚养管教，丫头哥的收入全部上交给大刘，大刘把家庭安排得有来倒去，皆大欢喜。

安稳的日子不长，一个一个的运动来了，丫头哥首当其冲受到批判，社会主义国家怎能允许有两个老婆？「地富反坏右」丫头哥属于哪类分子呢？结果坏分子的帽子顶在丫头哥头上。他自己反省，决定离婚，离掉大的还是小的？让他很纠结，于是决定两个都离掉。大小二刘也都无奈，各自带了自己的孩子各奔东西，一家人散伙了。

## Cousin "Yatou"
My grandfather made the decision to arrange a marriage for my eldest uncle. I had only heard that Matchmaker Yao, the most famous silver-tongued orator in the village, was a superb matchmaker. My uncle's marriage was just one of the many unions she had successfully brokered.

As soon as Matchmaker Yao stepped into my uncle’s courtyard, before she had even settled into her seat, she said with a grin, "I've come to ask for a cup of wedding wine." When the head of the household served her tea and a water pipe, her wrinkled face lit up with an animated, beaming expression. She spoke non-stop, praising how wonderful the girl's family was, saying that everyone within a ten-mile radius knew of the Zhao family's wealth—how much land, property, and business they owned. She also mentioned that the girl was educated, sensible, capable, and smart... in short, she listed every possible merit and exhausted all forms of praise.

When the bridesmaid handed the bride, whose head was covered in a red silk veil, over to my uncle, she said to the groom, "Bride, bride, from this day on, the bride is your new mother; she is here to look after your daily life." At that moment, the groom was beaming with joy, utterly beside himself with excitement and delight.

But when my uncle lifted the bride's red veil, his heart instantly sank. He widened his eyes to take a closer look, only to see a deep scar running from her forehead down to the bridge of her nose. The scar marred her entire face; even the most beautiful girl would have been rendered plain by it. Tensing up, my uncle took a sharp breath. It was as if he had been plunged from scorching heat into an ice house; all his passion evaporated.

Out of respect for my grandfather's authority, my uncle was forced to consummate the marriage with my aunt. Strangely enough, my aunt was quite capable. Over time, she completely tamed my uncle, who would bow, scrape, and smile obsequiously whenever he saw her. Over the next three years, my aunt gave birth to two boys. This made her hold her head even higher. Every penny of my uncle's salary had to be handed over; he dared not keep a single cent and became entirely submissive.

By some quirk of genetics, the two boys born to my uncle and aunt grew up to be tall, handsome, and cheerful. They had high-bridged noses, deep-set eyes with large, dark, and spirited pupils, and slightly curly hair that gave them a somewhat exotic look. This overjoyed my grandfather and uncle, who doted on them immensely. For some reason, they named the eldest boy "Yatou" (Little Girl), believing that a humble nickname would make him easier to raise.

During his high school years, Cousin Yatou’s handsome looks attracted the admiration of many female classmates. Some girls would secretly slip Parker fountain pens into his pencil case, others would press imported chocolates and butterscotch candies into his hands, and some would even stuff their own pocket money into his pockets...

In 1948, Cousin Yatou went to the National Southwestern Associated University along with a large group of students. In the summer of the following year, the vast majority of the university's students followed the Kuomintang (Nationalist Party) to Taiwan. At this critical juncture, Cousin Yatou came down with a high fever, reportedly contracting scarlet fever. Every evening, his temperature would spike to around 40 degrees Celsius, only to drop during the day. This cycle repeated for several weeks, leaving him unable to stand or walk. The other students were too busy packing their own bags and waiting for plane tickets to flee; no one had the time to care for him. Once the male and female students in the dormitory had practically all departed, Cousin Yatou was left to his fate, to survive or perish on his own.

Just as Cousin Yatou was burning up and slipping into unconsciousness, a female classmate appeared by his side. She placed cold towels on his forehead, and busied herself cooking porridge, pouring water, and fetching medicine for him. Her name was Liu Zhiling, and she was determined to stay behind to take care of him. Liu Zhiling, who had come to the university from Zhejiang, was kind and gentle, but her face was marred by several large pockmarks. Although she had noticed Cousin Yatou long ago, her self-consciousness about her appearance had kept her from approaching him. Now, she hoped to win his heart through her genuine devotion.

After more than a month of her painstaking care and meticulous attention, Cousin Yatou slowly recovered. With Liu Zhiling constantly by his side, feelings naturally developed over time. Grateful for her unwavering sincerity, Cousin Yatou agreed to be with her, and so the two became a couple.

With flights to Taiwan suspended and all those who could leave having already evacuated, Cousin Yatou and Liu Zhiling made their way to Shanghai. Cousin Yatou secured a good job, while Liu Zhiling became pregnant and gave birth to a son, followed soon after by a daughter. She stayed at home to raise the children and manage the household, and they lived a reasonably comfortable life.

Cousin Yatou took the bus to work, where he met a conductor named Liu Zhenxian. She was elegant, graceful, possessed an exceptionally sweet and moving voice, and radiated beauty. Cousin Yatou, for his part, was dashing and handsome, and their eyes instantly locked. He began catching this specific bus on time every single day, thoroughly enjoying his commute, and before long, he married the young conductor.

Liu Zhiling did not make a scene; she had long anticipated that Cousin Yatou would step out of line eventually. Cousin Yatou rented two adjacent apartments in Xiaonanmen so that the two "Lius" could live side-by-side. As for Cousin Yatou, he arranged to rotate between Big Liu's and Little Liu's apartments every week. Surprisingly, the two Lius lived in harmony. Cousin Yatou managed to secure a job for Little Liu as a clerk at his workplace. A few years later, Little Liu also gave birth to a son. This younger son was handed over to Big Liu to raise and discipline. All of Cousin Yatou’s income was handed over to Big Liu, who managed the household so systematically and fairly that everyone was perfectly content.

However, their peaceful days did not last. Political movements came one after another, and Cousin Yatou was the first to bear the brunt of the criticism. How could a socialist country tolerate a man having two wives? Among the "landlords, rich peasants, counter-revolutionaries, bad elements, and rightists," which category did Cousin Yatou belong to? Ultimately, he was branded a "bad element." Reflecting on his situation, he decided to get a divorce. But should he divorce the older one or the younger one? He was deeply torn, so he decided to divorce both of them. Left with no choice, both Big Liu and Little Liu took their respective children and went their separate ways, and the family disbanded.
