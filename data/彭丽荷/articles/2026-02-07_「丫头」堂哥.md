由祖父作主为大伯订了一门婚事。只听说村里的名嘴姚媒婆是顶呱呱的月老牵线人，大伯的婚姻就是姚媒婆牵线搭桥撮合的其中一对新人。

姚媒婆走进了大伯家的庭院，人还没有坐稳，就笑嘻嘻道：「我是来讨杯喜酒的。」当家人送上了茶水和水烟袋，皱巴巴的脸上展现出眉飞舞色、笑逐颜开的神态，满口夸奖女方家庭如何如何好，方圆十里都能知晓赵家的富有，田地、房产、生意有多少多少，并说女孩读过书、受过教育，懂事、能干、聪明……，反正优点报尽，好话说全。

喜娘把头盖红丝巾新娘子的手交给伯父时，对着新郎说：「新娘新娘，从今以后，新娘就是你新的娘，她来照顾你的生活起居的。」此时的新郎喜笑颜开，得意忘形，不知道有多少激动和兴奋。

当伯父把新娘子的红盖头捎开之后，顿时心中一沉，睁大眼睛仔细地看了看，只见新娘从额头到鼻梁处有一道很深的疤痕，疤痕影响了整个脸蛋，即使再漂亮的姑娘也会变丑。伯父一紧张，深深吸了口凉气，他从高温掉到冰库似的，什么激情都没有了。

由于祖父的威严，大伯被迫和伯母圆了房。说来奇怪，这个伯母还真有点本领，慢慢地制服了伯父，伯父见到伯母总是低头哈腰、陪笑说话。以后的三年中，伯母先后生下了两个男孩，这下伯母的腰板子挺得更直了，伯父赚来的工资如数上交，不敢留下一文钱，言听计从。

不知是什么基因，伯父和伯母俩生的两个男孩长得高大、帅气、阳光，高高的鼻梁、漥漥的眼眶中嵌着一双又大又黑有神的眸子，头发微微卷曲，带有点洋气，把祖父和大伯乐坏了，疼爱出奇。不知怎么想出来的，老大取名为「丫头」，说是叫得贱一点好养。

丫头哥高中年代，因帅气、英俊，惹来不少女同学的爱慕，有的女生把「派克」金笔悄悄地放进了丫头哥的铅笔盒里，有的女同学拿着进口的巧克力和奶油糖往他手里塞，更有女孩把自己的零花钱拽进了他的衣袋……。

一九四八年，丫头哥随着一大批学生来到了西南联大，第二年的夏天，西南联大的学生绝大多数随着[**国民党**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/国民党)去了台湾。在这骨节眼上，丫头哥发起了高烧，说是得了「猩红热」，每到傍晚体温就会上升，要烧到摄氏四十度左右，到了白天，温度又下降了。这样反复循环，持续了好几个星期，人也无法站立行走。其他的学生都自顾不暇地打好行装，等候[**机票**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/机票)，纷纷撤离，没有人能顾得上丫头哥。宿舍里的男女学生基本上都走完了，丫头哥只能听天由命，自生自灭。

当丫头哥烧得不省人事，他身边出现了一名女同学，拿了冷毛巾敷在他的前额，忙着帮他熬粥、倒水、拿药的。她叫刘智玲，她决心留下来照顾丫头哥。刘智玲是从浙江到西南联大的，性格善良温柔，只是脸上有好几颗[**大麻**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/大麻)子，影响了她的容貌，她早就注意到丫头哥，无奈自己美中不足，不敢接近，她想用真情实意打动他的心。

经过一个多月的艰辛照料、体贴入微的关心，丫头哥缓慢地恢复，而刘智玲始终陪伴左右，日久生情，丫头哥念她对自己一片真心，就这样两人在一起了。

去台湾的飞机已经停飞，该走的都撤完了。丫头哥和刘智玲两人来到上海，丫头哥找到了一份好差事，刘智玲怀孕生儿子，接着又迎来了女儿，她在家相夫教子，这样的日子真还可以。

丫头哥乘公交车上班，车上的一个卖票员名唤刘贞娴，长得秀丽端庄，声音特别甜美，动人可爱，光鲜照人。而丫头哥英俊潇洒，帅气十足，两人的眼神一下就对上了。丫头哥每天都准时乘上这班车，挺享受这段乘车的时间，不久就把卖票小姐娶回了家。

刘智玲不吵不闹，她也早料到丫头哥总有一天会出格的。丫头哥在小南门租借了紧挨的两间房子，让二个「刘」比邻而居。至于丫头哥，他安排自己每周轮换着去大刘和小刘的住处，二「刘」和睦相处，共度时光。丫头哥想办法把小刘安排到自己工作单位当了文书，数年之后，小刘也产下一子，这个小儿子交给大刘抚养管教，丫头哥的收入全部上交给大刘，大刘把家庭安排得有来倒去，皆大欢喜。

安稳的日子不长，一个一个的运动来了，丫头哥首当其冲受到批判，社会主义国家怎能允许有两个老婆？「地富反坏右」丫头哥属于哪类分子呢？结果坏分子的帽子顶在丫头哥头上。他自己反省，决定离婚，离掉大的还是小的？让他很纠结，于是决定两个都离掉。大小二刘也都无奈，各自带了自己的孩子各奔东西，一家人散伙了。

## English Translation
Cousin "Yatou"

Date: 2026-02-07 01:00 ET
Author: Peng Lihe

My grandfather took it upon himself to arrange a marriage for my eldest uncle. We had only heard that the village's famous, fast-talking Matchmaker Yao was an exceptional matchmaker, and my uncle's marriage was just one of the many unions she had successfully brought together.

When Matchmaker Yao walked into my uncle's courtyard, before she had even settled into her seat, she said with a grin, "I've come to ask for a cup of wedding wine!" When the head of the household served her tea and a water pipe, her wrinkled face lit up with an animated and radiant expression. She praised the bride's family to the skies, saying that everyone within a ten-mile radius knew of the Zhao family's wealth—how much land, property, and business they owned. She added that the girl was educated, sensible, capable, and clever... In short, she listed every possible merit and said nothing but good things.

When the wedding maid handed the bride—whose head was covered in a red silk veil—to my uncle, she said to the groom, "Bride, bride, from this day forward, the new bride is your new mother [a play on words in Chinese, as 'bride' sounds like 'new mother']; she is here to look after your daily life." At that moment, the groom was beaming with joy, beside himself with excitement and ecstasy.

But when my uncle swept aside the bride's red veil, his heart instantly sank. He widened his eyes and looked closely, only to see a deep scar running from her forehead to the bridge of her nose. The scar marred her entire face; even the most beautiful girl would have been rendered unsightly by it. Panicking, my uncle gasped. It felt as though he had been plunged from a hot furnace into an icehouse; all his passion evaporated.

Due to my grandfather's authority, my uncle was forced to consummate the marriage with my aunt. Strangely enough, this aunt of mine possessed quite some ability. She gradually tamed my uncle, who would bow, scrape, and smile obsequiously whenever he saw her. Over the next three years, my aunt gave birth to two boys. This allowed her to stand even taller. My uncle handed over his entire salary without keeping a single penny, obeying her every word.

By some quirk of genetics, the two boys born to my uncle and aunt grew up to be tall, handsome, and radiant. With high nose bridges, deep-set eyes housing large, bright, dark eyes, and slightly wavy hair, they had a somewhat foreign look. This delighted my grandfather and uncle to no end, and they doted on them extraordinarily. For reasons unknown, they named the eldest boy "Yatou" (meaning Little Girl or Maid), believing that giving a child a lowly name would make them easier to raise.

During his high school years, Yatou's handsome and dashing looks attracted the admiration of many female classmates. Some girls would quietly slip Parker fountain pens into his pencil case; others would stuff imported chocolates and cream candies into his hands; some even shoved their own pocket money into his coat pockets...

In 1948, Yatou, along with a large group of students, went to the Southwestern Associated University. The following summer, the vast majority of the university's students followed the Kuomintang to Taiwan. At this critical juncture, Yatou fell ill with a high fever. It was said to be scarlet fever. Every evening, his body temperature would spike to around 40 degrees Celsius, only to drop back down during the day. This cycle repeated for several weeks, leaving him unable to stand or walk. The other students, busy packing their own bags and waiting for plane tickets to evacuate, had no time to spare for anyone else. No one could look after Yatou. Once the male and female students in the dormitory had almost all left, Yatou could only resign himself to fate, left to survive or perish on his own.

As Yatou lay unconscious from the fever, a female classmate appeared by his side. She placed cold towels on his forehead and busied herself cooking porridge, pouring water, and fetching medicine for him. Her name was Liu Zhiling. She had resolved to stay behind to care for him. Liu Zhiling, who came to Southwestern Associated University from Zhejiang, was gentle and kind, but she had several large pockmarks on her face that detracted from her appearance. She had long noticed Yatou, but feeling self-conscious about her looks, she hadn't dared to approach him. She hoped to touch his heart with her genuine sincerity.

After more than a month of painstaking and meticulous care, Yatou slowly recovered. Throughout this time, Liu Zhiling remained constantly by his side. Over time, feelings blossomed between them. Grateful for her absolute devotion, Yatou reciprocated, and the two became a couple.

Flights to Taiwan had ceased, and those who were going to leave had already evacuated. Yatou and Liu Zhiling moved to Shanghai, where Yatou found a good job. Liu Zhiling gave birth to a son, followed by a daughter. She stayed home to care for her husband and raise the children, and they lived a reasonably comfortable life.

Yatou commuted to work by bus. A female conductor on the bus, named Liu Zhenxian, was beautiful, elegant, and possessed an exceptionally sweet, moving, and radiant voice. Meanwhile, Yatou was incredibly dashing and handsome, and their eyes locked instantly. Yatou began taking this bus on time every day, thoroughly enjoying his commute, and before long, he married the young conductor.

Liu Zhiling did not make a scene. She had long anticipated that Yatou would stray sooner or later. Yatou rented two adjacent rooms in Xiaonanmen so that the two "Lius" lived next door to each other. As for Yatou, he arranged to alternate weeks between Big Liu and Small Liu's homes. The two Lius lived in harmony and shared their lives. Yatou managed to secure a job for Small Liu as a clerk in his own work unit. A few years later, Small Liu also gave birth to a son, who was handed over to Big Liu to raise and educate. Yatou handed his entire income over to Big Liu, who managed the household so smoothly and meticulously that everyone was happy.

These stable days did not last. One political movement after another arrived, and Yatou was among the first to be targeted for criticism. How could a socialist country tolerate a man having two wives? Which category of the "landlords, rich peasants, counter-revolutionaries, bad elements, and rightists" did Yatou belong to? Ultimately, the label of "bad element" was pinned on his head. Reflecting on his situation, he decided to get a divorce. But which one should he divorce—the older one or the younger one? He was deeply conflicted, so he decided to divorce both. Powerless to do otherwise, both Big Liu and Small Liu went their separate ways, each taking their own children. The family was disbanded.
