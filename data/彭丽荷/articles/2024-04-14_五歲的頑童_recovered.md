# 五歲的頑童

**Date:** 2024-04-14 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭麗荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121252/7897901

麦浪是一个五岁的顽童，他聪明、活泼、可爱，很讨人喜爱。麦浪和父母住在美国西部[**圣地牙哥**](http://web.archive.org/web/20250425190300/https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego）的老城区（Old Town），一家三口过著安逸的生活。

每天吃完早餐，妈妈开车送他到学前班，他自己一个人雄纠纠、气昂昂地走进教室，不需要妈妈的陪同，一进教室就和老师打招呼或者做个鬼脸，逗老师开心。

老师是一个胖乎乎、笑咪咪、皮肤白晳的六十岁左右妇女，说她是学前班的老师，还不如说是一个慈祥、和蔼可亲的奶奶。她很喜爱麦浪，一见到孩子就会给他们一个拥抱和一个亲吻。铃声响起，小朋友按照自己的座位坐好，认真听老师讲故事，或者老师弹琴、唱歌给孩子们听，有时也会请小朋友拿起笔学著画图。老师看著孩子们都很认真、专心地画画，心中十分高兴。

突然间，老师发现麦浪不见了，他的座位上没有人，她赶忙环顾四周，又去厕所寻找，都没有看到麦浪。麦浪到底跑哪儿去了？她著急了，忙问其他小朋友有没有看到？一个小朋友用手指向一个大台子，告诉老师他在那儿。老师急忙上前俯身一看，居然看到麦浪妥妥地坐在台子底下，手拿一个平板电脑在玩游戏，老师叫他出来，他根本没有听到，还在那儿咧嘴开怀大笑。于是老师耐心地把麦浪抱出来，并对他说：「好孩子，等下课后或者回到家再玩，好吗？」麦浪躺在老师的怀里继续撒娇。

晚上，墙上的挂钟敲响了九下，妈妈叫麦浪回到自己的房间准备睡觉，漱洗完毕，妈妈看到麦浪爬上了床，帮他盖好被子，随手关上灯，把房门轻轻地带上。

大约十一点左右，大门的铃声响起，爸爸在睡梦中被惊醒，嘴里嘀咕著，半夜三更谁会来敲门？爸爸披上外套，把门打开，在昏暗的路灯下看到不远处停了几辆汽车，门口有好几个外卖人员正排著队，一个个手中托著扁平的方纸盒，送来了各种不同口味的披萨、冰淇淋还有苹果派。

「到底怎么回事？」爸爸疑惑地用手摸著脑袋。他对外卖人员说：「我们没有叫外卖，你们搞错了，我们晚上不需要这些食物。」外卖人员核对了门牌、地址，说道：「不会错的，我们收到了订单，你们已经付过款了。」

无奈之下，爸爸忽然想起了什么？他赶紧到麦浪的房间，此时麦浪正忙碌地拨动著手机，原来麦浪在手机上输入了他喜欢的各种食品。

爸爸把儿子叫到客厅，严厉地训斥麦浪，叫他好好反省。此时，门铃再次响起，儿子高声喊：「爸爸，快去开门，看看我喜欢的[**义大利**](http://web.archive.org/web/20250425190300/https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/义大利)披萨有没有送来？」还加了一句「你们别忘了付小费给外送的小哥哥。」老爸心想，我到底该哭还是该笑？

## The Five-Year-Old Mischief-Maker
Mailang is a five-year-old mischief-maker. He is smart, lively, cute, and very lovable. Mailang and his parents live in the Old Town of San Diego, in the western United States, where the family of three lives a comfortable and peaceful life.

Every day after breakfast, his mother drives him to preschool. He marches into the classroom on his own, full of mettle and spirit, without needing his mother's accompaniment. As soon as he enters, he greets the teacher or makes a face to make her laugh.

The teacher is a chubby, smiling woman of about sixty with fair skin. Rather than calling her a preschool teacher, it would be better to describe her as a kind, amiable grandmother. She is very fond of Mailang, and whenever she sees the children, she gives them a hug and a kiss. When the bell rings, the children sit in their respective seats and listen attentively as the teacher tells stories, or as she plays the piano and sings to them. Sometimes, she also asks them to pick up their crayons and learn to draw. Watching the children drawing so earnestly and intently, the teacher feels extremely happy.

Suddenly, the teacher notices that Mailang is gone; his seat is empty. She quickly looks around and checks the restroom, but Mailang is nowhere to be seen. Where on earth did Mailang go? Becoming anxious, she hurriedly asks the other children if they have seen him. One of the children points to a large platform, telling the teacher he is over there. The teacher quickly steps forward, bends down to look, and unexpectedly finds Mailang sitting comfortably underneath the platform, playing games on a tablet. When the teacher calls for him to come out, he doesn't hear her at all and just sits there grinning. So, the teacher patiently pulls Mailang out and says to him, "Good boy, wait until after class or when you get home to play, okay?" Mailang just snuggles in the teacher's arms, continuing to act spoiled.

In the evening, the wall clock strikes nine. His mother tells Mailang to go to his room and get ready for bed. After he finishes washing up, she sees him climb into bed, helps him tuck in, switches off the light, and gently closes the door behind her.

Around eleven o'clock, the doorbell rings. Awakened from his sleep, his father mutters to himself, wondering who could be knocking on the door in the middle of the night. Slipping on a coat, the father opens the door. Under the dim streetlights, he sees several cars parked not far away, and several delivery people standing in line at the entrance. Each of them is holding flat, square cardboard boxes, delivering pizzas of various flavors, ice cream, and apple pies.

"What on earth is going on?" the father scratches his head in confusion. He says to the delivery drivers, "We didn't order any takeout. You must have made a mistake. We don't need this food tonight." The delivery drivers double-check the house number and address, saying, "There's no mistake. We received the order, and it has already been paid for."

Feeling helpless, the father suddenly realizes something. He rushes to Mailang's room, where Mailang is busy tapping away on a smartphone. As it turns out, Mailang had ordered all his favorite foods through the phone.

The father calls his son to the living room, sternly reprimanding him and telling him to reflect on his actions. Just then, the doorbell rings again. The boy shouts loudly, "Dad, hurry up and open the door! See if my favorite Italian pizza has arrived yet?" He adds, "Don't forget to tip the delivery guy!" His dad thinks to himself, "Should I laugh or should I cry?"
