# 阿米哥

**Date:** 2025-12-02 01:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121252/9163276?zh-cn

「阿米哥（Amigo）」，是[**墨西哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/墨西哥)人对朋友的一种尊重而友好的称呼，初次见面叫一声「朋友」，心中就有了一股浓浓的暖意。真没料到，到美国三十多年，竟与一个名叫「班」的阿米哥成为好朋友。

三十多年前，女儿、女婿刚在美国站稳脚跟，他们在[**圣地牙哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego）买了一幢独立的小房子，三房二卫。房子虽小，但是后花园倒挺大的，有高大的芭蕉树、杨柳树、椰子树、宽广的草坪，后面还有个小山坡。

秋天树叶泛黄，秋叶吹落在整个后院，枯萎的树枝也垂落挂在树干上，草坪上的野草、杂草和青草参差不齐地冒在阳光下，小山坡上也是杂草丛生、一片乱象。

女儿说，这种萎靡不振、杂乱糟糕的环境得赶快收拾一下。那时，他俩收入不多，家境不富裕，为了节省费用，只能自己动手打理这些粗活。有一天，女儿、女婿换上了破旧的劳动服，卷起了袖子准备大干一番，当女婿推起割草机，他使出了吃奶的力气，可是割草机就是不听使唤，不愿意工作。原来是女婿没有拉开燃油箱，割草机怎么肯走呢？好不容易把草割完，女婿坐在一边，手拿一块干毛巾，在脸上、脖子上狠狠地擦拭着，喝了几口水，又举起了沉甸甸的长柄剪刀向枯黄的树枝发力。没剪几次，他的双手就支撑不住了，汗水从额前往下直淌，女婿用发软的手臂扶正眼镜，但手指不听命令，眼镜「哐啷」一声坠落在地，镜片碎了。

女儿气呼呼地责怪女婿不小心，然后费劲地把地上的杂草、烂叶以及几根枯枝装进垃圾桶，草草收兵，二人精疲力尽，像泄了气的皮球，瘫软在沙发上。

隔天两人全身筋骨酸痛，一周后才逐渐恢复。女婿说：「这割草的活我干不了，几十块钱不能省。」从此，「阿米哥」班每周一次来收拾后院，他为人老实又精力旺盛，干活敏捷，动作利索，不到半小时的时间，就把前庭后院打扫得干净整洁。四周的树木修剪得整齐有序，树枝上的花朶在树梢上争艳斗色地展显自我，草地一片绿油油，令人喜欢。

每次班收拾好工具离去时，我总会拿点水果或小点心慰劳他，叫他休息一下再走，他总是满脸笑容地摇摇手。久而之久，我们渐渐互相了解，不知不觉间默默地成了朋友。

一天早晨，来割草的不是班，而是他的儿子强，今年才十六岁。强告诉我们，他的老爸爬到一棵高大的树上整理树枝，一不小心，连人带树枝摔倒在地，二根肋骨骨折，而且刺伤了肺叶。强又说，以后这里的活由他来做，等他爸恢复了再来。我们知道后，心中为班担忧，女儿买了水果和恢复骨骼的营养品，让强带回去转交给他父亲。

往后的岁月里，都由强担任我们家的庭园工作，班只能干点轻微的小活。一天，班出现在后花园，他弯着腰默默修理着草地上的喷水龙头，我们上前和他招呼，并要付钱给他，但无论如何他不接受，只说：小事小事。无奈之下，我们只能悄悄地给强涨工资。

后来我们买了[**学区**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/学区)房，要搬家了，强心里非常难过。女婿打趣地说：「老房子出租，你照旧打理，我们会把你捆绑在一起带过去的。」在新房子的花园里，我们又见面了，我们和强脸上都泛出了笑容。

## Amigo
"Amigo" is a respectful and friendly term that Mexicans use for "friend." Hearing "friend" at a first meeting brings a deep sense of warmth to one's heart. I never expected that after living in the United States for over thirty years, I would become close friends with an "amigo" named Ben.

More than thirty years ago, when my daughter and son-in-law had just established a foothold in the United States, they bought a small, detached house in San Diego with three bedrooms and two bathrooms. Although the house was small, the backyard was quite large, featuring tall banana, willow, and coconut trees, a wide lawn, and a small hillside in the back.

In the autumn, the leaves turned yellow and drifted across the entire backyard. Withered branches hung limply from the tree trunks. Wild grass, weeds, and green grass sprouted unevenly under the sun, and the small hillside was overgrown with weeds, presenting a scene of complete chaos.

My daughter said that this gloomy, messy, and terrible environment had to be cleaned up quickly. At that time, their income was modest, and their family was not wealthy. To save money, they had no choice but to do this heavy labor themselves. One day, my daughter and son-in-law changed into old work clothes and rolled up their sleeves, ready to work hard. When my son-in-law pushed the lawnmower, he exerted every ounce of his strength, but the lawnmower refused to cooperate and wouldn't start. It turned out he hadn't opened the fuel tank valve—how could the mower run? After finally finishing the mowing with great difficulty, my son-in-law sat to one side, holding a dry towel, wiping his face and neck vigorously. After taking a few sips of water, he picked up a pair of heavy, long-handled shears to tackle the withered branches. After only a few snips, his arms could no longer support the weight. Sweat poured down from his forehead. He tried to adjust his glasses with his weak arms, but his fingers disobeyed, and with a "clink," the glasses fell to the ground, shattering the lenses.

Angrily, my daughter blamed him for being careless. She then painstakingly packed the weeds, decaying leaves, and a few dead branches into the trash can. Calling it a day prematurely, they were both thoroughly exhausted, looking like deflated balloons as they collapsed limply onto the sofa.

The next day, their entire bodies were sore and aching, and they only gradually recovered a week later. My son-in-law said, "I can't do this mowing work; we can't save these tens of dollars." From then on, "Amigo" Ben came once a week to tidy up the backyard. He was honest, energetic, quick, and efficient. In less than half an hour, he would sweep and clean both the front and back yards. The surrounding trees were pruned in an orderly fashion, the flowers on the branches competed in brilliance to showcase themselves, and the lawn became a vibrant, lovely green.

Every time Ben packed his tools to leave, I would always bring out some fruit or snacks to reward him, telling him to rest a bit before going. He would always wave his hand with a face full of smiles. Over time, we gradually got to know each other, and without realizing it, we silently became friends.

One morning, it was not Ben who came to mow the lawn, but his son, John, who was only sixteen years old. John told us that his dad had climbed a tall tree to trim branches and had accidentally fallen to the ground along with the branches, fracturing two ribs and puncturing his lung. John added that he would handle the work here from now on until his dad recovered. Upon hearing this, we worried deeply for Ben. My daughter bought some fruit and supplements for bone recovery, asking John to take them back to his father.

In the years that followed, John took over our yard work, and Ben could only do light chores. One day, Ben appeared in the backyard, bending over to silently repair a lawn sprinkler. We went over to greet him and tried to pay him, but no matter what, he refused, simply saying, "It's a small thing, a small thing." Having no other choice, we could only quietly raise John's wages.

Later, we bought a house in a good school district and were about to move, which made John very sad. My son-in-law joked, "We are renting out the old house, and you will continue to maintain it. We'll tie you up and bring you along with us!" In the garden of the new house, we met again, and smiles broke out on both our faces and John's.
