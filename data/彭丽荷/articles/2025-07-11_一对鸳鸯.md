# 一对鸳鸯

**Date:** 2025-07-11 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121252/8850205?zh-cn

年已过、春又回，阳光明媚，万物苏醒。我们家住在[**加州**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/加州)[**圣地牙哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego,California）的一条街上，前庭后院每棵树尖上冒出了浅浅绿色的小嫩芽，每天变个样，争先恐后地似乎在比赛谁长得更快更绿？躲藏在小嫩叶底下的花蕾也不甘落后，一朵朵娇滴滴、羞答答含笑的花苞偷偷地等着绽放。

一天，孙女要她爸爸带她去圣地牙哥米拉马水库（San Diego Miramar Lake）公园游玩，那儿有一个很大的天然湖泊，有不少鸭子、天鹅、鸳鸯和许多不知名的水鸟。孙女每次去公园总是兴致勃勃，出发前，她为鸭子、天鹅、鸳鸯带上面包、饼干、水果和胡萝卜。这次去公园，还特地邀我同行。

从不远处，我们就看到一个很宽阔的湖泊，碧绿的水面随着风儿荡漾，只见身着花衣的鸭子、白色羽毛的天鹅、美丽的鸳鸯都浮在水面上，像一艘艘小[**快艇**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/快艇)自由自在悠闲划动着。孙女把食物扔向这些鸟儿，瞬时，所有的鸟儿都朝着孙女争先恐后地游过来。孙女高兴地拍起了小手，鸟儿啄食和孙女愉快的笑容，像是一幅美丽的油画。

突然，一道暗影划破了这宁静、美好的画面，一只老鹰急转而下，企图从湖泊里攫捉水鸟。水鸟们突然受到惊吓，急忙四处窜逃，两只天鹅反应迅速，搧开左右翅膀，立马跃到对面的岸上。孙女被这恐怖的埸景吓得哇哇大哭，儿子赶快将她搂在怀里，安慰着说：「别害怕，有爸爸呢。」

还正说着，只见那只老鹰抓捕未成功，仍不罢休，重新飞上天空，第二次俯身冲下来，目标锁定一只雌鸳鸯。鸳鸯和其他水鸟都吓得不敢起飞，眼见那只雌鸳鸯面临危险，旁边的雄鸳鸯见状，立即紧急窜过来，张开双翅，勇敢、顽强地搧打俯冲下来的老鹰。结果老鹰第二次袭击也没有成功，气急败坏地用硬梆梆的尖嘴，在雄鸳鸯的头上猛力撕咬，雄鸳鸯的头冠被啄得零乱不堪，然而老鹰又飞上空中，准备进行第三次攫捉，我们在一旁也看得胆战心惊。

当老鹰第三次使尽全力俯冲而来，此时，雄鸳鸯已不顾安危，把翅膀展开覆盖在雌鸳鸯的背上，全力保卫着雌鸳鸯。准备牺牲的雄鸳鸯和凶恶的老鹰，展开了力量悬殊的战斗，但雄鸳鸯哪是老鹰的对手？此时，天鹅、水鸟愤怒地游到老鹰身旁，各自展开大翅助阵，湖泊里的情况一片混乱。

儿子从地上捡起石头，狠狠地砸向老鹰，倔强的老鹰还在盘旋挣扎，孙女见状也鼓起勇气，帮忙捡起更多石块，递到爸爸手里，并大声地叫道：「快把这个坏蛋赶走。」

在群鸟的围攻之下，老鹰灰头土脸、一无所获，猛烈飞起，不知去向，只剩水面上羽毛零落一片。那只雄鸳鸯遍体鳞伤，二只眼睛紧紧地闭着，只见雌鸳鸯展开了右翅，轻轻搭在雄鸳鸯的身上，在湖泊边停留着。

过了一周，我们又站在湖泊边。只见雄鸳鸯往日的威风漂亮不再，牠的背上、翅根部有几处地方裸着褐色的创疤，而那只雌鸳鸯紧紧地依偎在雄鸳鸯的身旁。

## A Pair of Mandarin Ducks
The new year has passed, and spring has returned. The sun shines brightly, and all nature is waking up. Our family lives on a street in San Diego, California. In our front and back yards, light green buds have sprouted from the tips of every tree branch, changing day by day, as if competing to see who can grow faster and greener. The flower buds hiding beneath the tiny young leaves refuse to be left behind either; one by one, delicate and shyly smiling blossoms are secretly waiting to bloom.

One day, my granddaughter asked her father to take her to Miramar Lake Park in San Diego. There is a large natural lake there, home to many ducks, swans, mandarin ducks, and various unknown water birds. My granddaughter is always full of enthusiasm whenever she goes to the park. Before setting off, she prepared bread, cookies, fruits, and carrots for the ducks, swans, and mandarin ducks. This time, she specially invited me to go along.

From not far away, we could see a wide lake. Its emerald-green water rippled with the wind. Ducks in colorful coats, white-feathered swans, and beautiful mandarin ducks floated on the surface, gliding leisurely and freely like little speedboats. My granddaughter tossed food to these birds, and instantly, all of them swam eagerly toward her, vying with one another. She clapped her little hands happily. The birds pecking at the food and my granddaughter's joyful smile looked just like a beautiful oil painting.

Suddenly, a dark shadow pierced this peaceful and beautiful scene. An eagle swooped down, attempting to snatch a water bird from the lake. Startled, the birds scattered in panic. Two swans reacted quickly, flapping their wings and leaping onto the opposite shore. Terrified by this horrifying scene, my granddaughter burst into tears. My son quickly held her in his arms, comforting her: 'Don't be afraid, Daddy is here.'

Even as he spoke, the eagle, having failed in its first attempt, refused to give up. It soared back into the sky and swooped down a second time, locking its target on a female mandarin duck. The mandarin ducks and other water birds were too terrified to fly. Seeing the female mandarin duck in grave danger, the male mandarin duck next to her immediately rushed over, spread his wings, and bravely and tenaciously flapped against the descending eagle. As a result, the eagle's second attack failed as well. Flustered and exasperated, the eagle used its rock-hard, sharp beak to bite and tear savagely at the male's head. The male's crest was pecked into a disheveled mess. Yet, the eagle soared into the air again, preparing for a third strike, leaving us watching from the side in sheer terror.

As the eagle swooped down with all its might for the third time, the male mandarin duck, completely disregarding his own safety, spread his wings to cover the female's back, defending her with everything he had. Ready to make the ultimate sacrifice, the male mandarin duck engaged in a highly lopsided battle against the ferocious eagle. But how could a mandarin duck be a match for an eagle? At this moment, the swans and other water birds angrily swam over, spreading their wings to join the fight. The lake fell into utter chaos.

My son picked up a stone from the ground and hurled it fiercely at the eagle. The stubborn eagle was still circling and struggling. Seeing this, my granddaughter also plucked up her courage, helping to pick up more stones to hand to her father, shouting loudly, 'Hurry and drive this bad guy away!'

Under the siege of the birds, the eagle, defeated and empty-handed, took off abruptly and disappeared, leaving only a scattering of feathers floating on the water. The male mandarin duck was covered in wounds, both eyes tightly shut. The female mandarin duck spread her right wing, gently resting it on his body, as they lingered by the edge of the lake.

A week later, we stood by the lake again. The male mandarin duck's former majestic beauty was gone; several bare, brown scars were visible on his back and the base of his wings. Yet, the female mandarin duck snuggled closely by his side.
