# 一对鸳鸯

**Date:** 2025-07-11 02:00 ET
**Author:** 彭丽荷

年已过、春又回，阳光明媚，万物苏醒。我们家住在[**加州**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/加州)[**圣地牙哥**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/圣地牙哥)（San Diego,California）的一条街上，前庭后院每棵树尖上冒出了浅浅绿色的小嫩芽，每天变个样，争先恐后地似乎在比赛谁长得更快更绿？躲藏在小嫩叶底下的花蕾也不甘落后，一朵朵娇滴滴、羞答答含笑的花苞偷偷地等着绽放。

一天，孙女要她爸爸带她去圣地牙哥米拉马水库（San Diego Miramar Lake）公园游玩，那儿有一个很大的天然湖泊，有不少鸭子、天鹅、鸳鸯和许多不知名的水鸟。孙女每次去公园总是兴致勃勃，出发前，她为鸭子、天鹅、鸳鸯带上面包、饼干、水果和胡萝卜。这次去公园，还特地邀我同行。

从不远处，我们就看到一个很宽阔的湖泊，碧绿的水面随着风儿荡漾，只见身着花衣的鸭子、白色羽毛的天鹅、美丽的鸳鸯都浮在水面上，像一艘艘小[**快艇**](https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/快艇)自由自在悠闲划动着。孙女把食物扔向这些鸟儿，瞬时，所有的鸟儿都朝着孙女争先恐后地游过来。孙女高兴地拍起了小手，鸟儿啄食和孙女愉快的笑容，像是一幅美丽的油画。

突然，一道暗影划破了这宁静、美好的画面，一只老鹰急转而下，企图从湖泊里攫捉水鸟。水鸟们突然受到惊吓，急忙四处窜逃，两只天鹅反应迅速，搧开左右翅膀，立马跃到对面的岸上。孙女被这恐怖的埸景吓得哇哇大哭，儿子赶快将她搂在怀里，安慰着说：「别害怕，有爸爸呢。」

还正说着，只见那只老鹰抓捕未成功，仍不罢休，重新飞上天空，第二次俯身冲下来，目标锁定一只雌鸳鸯。鸳鸯和其他水鸟都吓得不敢起飞，眼见那只雌鸳鸯面临危险，旁边的雄鸳鸯见状，立即紧急窜过来，张开双翅，勇敢、顽强地搧打俯冲下来的老鹰。结果老鹰第二次袭击也没有成功，气急败坏地用硬梆梆的尖嘴，在雄鸳鸯的头上猛力撕咬，雄鸳鸯的头冠被啄得零乱不堪，然而老鹰又飞上空中，准备进行第三次攫捉，我们在一旁也看得胆战心惊。

当老鹰第三次使尽全力俯冲而来，此时，雄鸳鸯已不顾安危，把翅膀展开覆盖在雌鸳鸯的背上，全力保卫着雌鸳鸯。准备牺牲的雄鸳鸯和凶恶的老鹰，展开了力量悬殊的战斗，但雄鸳鸯哪是老鹰的对手？此时，天鹅、水鸟愤怒地游到老鹰身旁，各自展开大翅助阵，湖泊里的情况一片混乱。

儿子从地上捡起石头，狠狠地砸向老鹰，倔强的老鹰还在盘旋挣扎，孙女见状也鼓起勇气，帮忙捡起更多石块，递到爸爸手里，并大声地叫道：「快把这个坏蛋赶走。」

在群鸟的围攻之下，老鹰灰头土脸、一无所获，猛烈飞起，不知去向，只剩水面上羽毛零落一片。那只雄鸳鸯遍体鳞伤，二只眼睛紧紧地闭着，只见雌鸳鸯展开了右翅，轻轻搭在雄鸳鸯的身上，在湖泊边停留着。

过了一周，我们又站在湖泊边。只见雄鸳鸯往日的威风漂亮不再，牠的背上、翅根部有几处地方裸着褐色的创疤，而那只雌鸳鸯紧紧地依偎在雄鸳鸯的身旁。

## English Translation
The year has passed, and spring has returned. The sun shines brightly, and all of nature is awakening. Our family lives on a street in San Diego, California. In our front and back yards, light green, tender little buds have emerged at the tips of every tree branch, changing day by day, eagerly competing as if to see who can grow faster and greener. The flower buds hiding beneath the tender young leaves refuse to be left behind either; delicate, shy, and smiling, they secretly wait to bloom.

One day, my granddaughter asked her father to take her to Miramar Lake Park in San Diego. There is a large natural lake there, home to many ducks, swans, mandarin ducks, and various unknown waterbirds. My granddaughter is always full of enthusiasm every time she goes to the park. Before setting off, she prepared bread, cookies, fruit, and carrots for the ducks, swans, and mandarin ducks. On this trip, she specially invited me to go along.

From not far away, we saw a very wide lake, its emerald-green surface rippling with the wind. We could see ducks with colorful patterns, white-feathered swans, and beautiful mandarin ducks floating on the water, gliding freely and leisurely like little speedboats. My granddaughter threw food to these birds, and instantly, all of them swam toward her, eagerly competing for the food. My granddaughter clapped her small hands joyfully. The birds pecking at the food and my granddaughter's happy smile looked just like a beautiful oil painting.

Suddenly, a dark shadow shattered this peaceful and beautiful scene. An eagle swooped down in a sharp turn, attempting to seize a waterbird from the lake. The waterbirds were suddenly startled and scrambled to escape in all directions. Two swans reacted quickly, spreading their wings and immediately leaping onto the opposite bank. My granddaughter was so frightened by this terrifying scene that she burst into tears. My son quickly held her in his arms, comforting her: 'Don't be afraid, Daddy is here.'

Even as he spoke, the eagle, having failed in its attempt, refused to give up. It soared back into the sky and swooped down a second time, locking its target onto a female mandarin duck. The mandarin duck and the other waterbirds were too terrified to fly. Seeing the female mandarin duck in grave danger, the nearby male mandarin duck immediately rushed over, spread his wings, and courageously and tenaciously beat back the swooping eagle. As a result, the eagle's second attack failed as well. In a fit of rage, it used its hard, sharp beak to viciously bite and tear at the male mandarin duck's head. The male's crest was pecked into a messy, chaotic state. Yet, the eagle flew back into the sky, preparing for a third attempt, leaving us watching from the side in sheer terror.

When the eagle swooped down with all its might for the third time, the male mandarin duck, completely disregarding his own safety, spread his wings to cover the female's back, protecting her with all his strength. The male mandarin duck, prepared to sacrifice himself, engaged in a highly lopsided battle with the ferocious eagle. But how could a mandarin duck be a match for an eagle? At this moment, the swans and waterbirds angrily swam to the eagle's side, spreading their large wings to join the fight, throwing the lake into utter chaos.

My son picked up stones from the ground and hurled them fiercely at the eagle. The stubborn eagle was still circling and struggling. Seeing this, my granddaughter also plucked up her courage, helping to pick up more stones to hand to her father while shouting loudly, 'Quick, drive this bad guy away!'

Under the joint attack of the birds, the eagle, humiliated and empty-handed, flew up violently and disappeared, leaving only scattered feathers floating on the water. The male mandarin duck was covered in wounds, both eyes tightly shut. The female mandarin duck spread her right wing, gently resting it on the male's body, as they lingered by the edge of the lake.

A week later, we stood by the lake again. The male mandarin duck's former majestic and beautiful appearance was gone; several bare, brown scars were visible on his back and the base of his wings. Meanwhile, the female mandarin duck snuggled closely by his side.
