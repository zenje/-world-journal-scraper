# 善良的女孩

**Date:** 2024-07-15 02:02 ET
**Author:** 彭麗荷
**URL:** https://www.worldjournal.com/wj/story/121252/8094190

每天傍晚六点左右，八十八岁的威廉老先生总会出现在圣地亚哥（San Diego）南面市中心街旁的一家餐馆，在餐馆右侧靠窗的桌旁坐好。他会把一根拐杖稳妥地勾在椅子的靠背后方，转过身，手中拿起一份当天的报纸，餐桌上总放著一副老花眼镜。

当威廉缓慢地跨进餐馆，他总会欠著身子示意，和不相识来就餐的顾客打招呼：「大家好，很高兴能见到你们。」威廉非常有礼貌，像老朋友一样问候大家，还会询问：「今天有什么新闻吗？有什么有趣的故事呢？可以讲给我们听。」看来威廉是一个性格开朗、特别喜欢讲话的长者。

威廉找到自己习惯的座位，他环顾四周，店里的客人不少，每个角落都洋溢著热闹气氛。他笑嘻嘻地和旁边的客人点头说道：「我们会度过一个美好的夜晚。」

没过多久，一个约十六、七岁女孩面带笑容走到威廉的座位旁，询问威廉：「先生您好，您需要点什么？想喝点什么？这是今天的菜单，请您慢慢看，我会马上过来，等您想好了，告诉我好吗？」女孩一边递给威廉菜单，一边亲切地说著。威廉看到小女孩活泼可爱，一双碧蓝的大眼睛更增添了她的灵气。

一会儿，女孩走到威廉身旁，轻声细语地问道：「先生，您想好了吗？要点什么？您可以点菜了。」威廉没有回答她的问题，却问道：「妳叫什么名字？怎么年纪这么小，却在这里工作？」

女孩微笑著说：「我叫爱丽丝，是Torrey Pines High School高中二年级的学生，每天完成当天的课业，就会准时到这家餐馆打工，今天很高兴能帮助您。」威廉高兴地说：「爱丽丝，这名字很优美，从今天开始我们就是好朋友了，我每天晚上会来这家餐馆吃饭。」

不久，威廉的餐点和啤酒都送来了，爱丽丝关心地说道：「您慢慢吃，有什么需要帮忙的尽管吩咐我。」威廉看到爱丽丝做事仔细、俐落，讲话又讨人喜欢，感到很温暖，心想自己如果有这样的女儿，那该有多好。

餐馆里的顾客陆续用完餐，逐渐离开了，墙上的时钟敲响了九下，威廉还没有想回家的意愿。店里的客人剩下不多，爱丽丝趁著空闲时间，走到威廉桌旁，她轻声细语地问道：「您家住得远吗？为什么不早点回家，家里有人在等您吗？」

当爱丽丝得知威廉是个孤独的老人，虽然他性格开朗、善于言谈，但一个人难免会感到孤单和空虚，看来找人讲话是他最大的爱好和乐趣。

从此威廉变成是餐馆的老顾客，每天傍晚，爱丽丝的眼光总会投向那个靠窗的座位，看威廉是否坐在那里？只要威廉笑著向她挥手示意，她的内心就感到踏实了。

爱丽丝时常无微不至地关心威廉，她的言语和行动带给威廉心灵上的温暖，一老一少培养出真挚的情谊，就这样，一年多过去了。

有一天晚上八点多，靠窗的座位一直空著，爱丽丝有点著急，赶忙抽空打电话去威廉家，可是电话那头没有回音。爱丽丝正忙著工作，无法抽身去威廉家中查看，她急忙打电话给警察，请求警察立即去威廉家关照，看一下是否有什么特殊情况？

当警察赶往威廉的家，只见威廉倒在浴室的地砖上，他安详地走了。爱丽丝得知后非常伤心，她暗自流泪，责怪自己没有照顾好威廉，对不起这个老人家。两星期后，有两个警察来到餐馆，他们找到爱丽丝，拿出了一张写有爱丽丝名字的五万美元[**支票**](http://web.archive.org/web/20250216082418/https://www.worldjournal.com/search/tagging/8877/支票)，警察把支票放到爱丽丝的手中并说道：「爱丽丝，这是妳应该得到的支票。」

## The Kind Girl
Every evening around six o'clock, eighty-eight-year-old Mr. William would always appear at a restaurant near the downtown area in south San Diego, sitting at a table near the window on the right side of the restaurant. He would hook a cane securely behind the back of his chair, turn around, and pick up a copy of the day's newspaper in his hand, with a pair of reading glasses always placed on the table.

As William slowly stepped into the restaurant, he would always lean forward to greet the unfamiliar customers who had come to dine: 'Hello everyone, it's very nice to meet you.' William was extremely polite, greeting everyone like old friends, and would also ask, 'Is there any news today? Any interesting stories? You can tell us.' It seemed that William was an outgoing senior who especially loved to talk.

William found his usual seat and looked around. There were many customers in the shop, and every corner was filled with a lively atmosphere. He nodded smilingly to the customers next to him and said, 'We will have a wonderful evening.'

Before long, a girl of about sixteen or seventeen walked to William's seat with a smile on her face and asked him, 'Hello, sir, what would you like to order? What would you like to drink? Here is today's menu, please take your time looking at it, and I will be right back. Once you've decided, would you please let me know?' The girl spoke warmly while handing the menu to William. William saw that the young girl was lively and lovely, and her large, clear blue eyes added to her quick-witted charm.

In a moment, the girl walked up to William and asked in a soft, gentle voice, 'Sir, have you decided? What would you like to order? You can order now.' William did not answer her question, but asked instead, 'What is your name? Why are you working here at such a young age?'

The girl smiled and said, 'My name is Alice. I am a sophomore at Torrey Pines High School. Every day, after completing my schoolwork for the day, I come to work at this restaurant on time. I am very happy to help you today.' William said happily, 'Alice, that's a beautiful name. From today on, we are good friends, and I will come to eat at this restaurant every evening.'

Soon, William's food and beer were delivered, and Alice said caringly, 'Please take your time. If you need any help, just let me know.' Seeing that Alice did things carefully and efficiently, and spoke in such a pleasing manner, William felt very warm inside, thinking to himself how wonderful it would be if he had a daughter like her.

The customers in the restaurant finished their meals one after another and gradually left. The clock on the wall struck nine, but William still had no intention of going home. There were not many customers left in the restaurant, so Alice took advantage of her free time to walk over to William's table. She asked in a soft, gentle voice, 'Do you live far away? Why don't you go home earlier? Is there anyone waiting for you at home?'

When Alice learned that William was a lonely old man—even though he was cheerful and talkative, he inevitably felt lonely and empty living alone—it seemed that finding someone to talk to was his greatest hobby and pleasure.

From then on, William became a regular customer at the restaurant. Every evening, Alice's eyes would always drift toward that window seat to see if William was sitting there. As long as William smiled and waved at her, she felt at ease.

Alice often showed meticulous care for William, and her words and actions brought warmth to William's soul. The old and the young developed a sincere friendship, and just like that, more than a year passed.

One evening around eight o'clock, the window seat remained empty. Alice got a bit anxious and took a moment to call William's house, but there was no answer on the other end. Busy with work, Alice couldn't leave to check on William's house herself, so she quickly called the police, asking them to go to William's house immediately to see if anything was wrong.

When the police arrived at William's house, they found him lying on the bathroom floor tiles, having passed away peacefully. Alice was heartbroken when she heard the news. She wept silently, blaming herself for not taking better care of William and feeling that she had let the old man down. Two weeks later, two police officers came to the restaurant. They found Alice, took out a check for fifty thousand dollars with Alice's name on it, placed the check in her hand, and said, 'Alice, this is a check you deserve.'
